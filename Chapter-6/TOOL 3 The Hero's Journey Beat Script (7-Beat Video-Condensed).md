## TOOL 3: The Hero's Journey Beat Script (7-Beat Video-Condensed)

### *For personal story arcs, transformation narratives, and "here's what I learned" content. Seven beats, each with a mandatory emotional job.*

---

```
═══ FRAMEWORK: HERO'S JOURNEY (Video-Condensed, 7 Beats) ═══
═══ STRUCTURAL SKELETON PROMPT ═══

TOPIC: [your specific topic / the transformation]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [who's watching]
THE "ORDINARY WORLD": [the before-state — what life looked like
before the journey]
THE CALL / DISRUPTION: [what changed — the event, decision,
or realization that started the journey]
THE ORDEAL: [the hardest moment — the failure, the doubt, the
breaking point]
THE TRANSFORMATION: [what changed — the insight, skill, or
perspective gained]
THE ELIXIR: [the universal lesson the viewer can apply]
VIDEO LENGTH TARGET: [___ minutes / ~___ words]

Write a complete script skeleton following this EXACT beat
sequence. Execute each beat as specified. Do NOT skip, merge,
or summarize.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 1: ORDINARY WORLD
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create identification. The viewer sees themselves
in the "before" state.

Content requirement: Establish the relatable starting point.
Concrete, sensory details of the "normal" life before the
disruption. What did a typical day/week look like? What did
the protagonist (you or your subject) believe about themselves?

Mandatory field: One specific routine, habit, or belief that
defines the "before" state. ("Every morning I'd [specific action]
and tell myself [specific belief].")

Prompt instruction: "Show the 'before' life in 3–4 concrete
details. Not 'I was unhappy.' What did the unhappiness LOOK
like? The specific morning routine. The specific lie told at
dinner parties. The specific app open on the phone at 11 p.m.
Make the viewer nod: 'That's me.'"

Length: [2 paragraphs / ~100–130 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 2: CALL TO ADVENTURE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create disruption. The comfortable world cracks.
The viewer feels the pull of "something has to change."

Content requirement: The specific event, encounter, or realization
that disrupts the ordinary world. A moment, not a gradual shift.
A Tuesday afternoon, not "over time."

Mandatory field: A specific inciting incident with a time, place,
or trigger. ("It was a Thursday. I was [doing X] when [Y happened].")

Prompt instruction: "Write the disruption as a SCENE. A specific
moment. What was happening? What changed? Include one line of
dialogue or internal thought that marks the before/after boundary.
'That's when I realized...' or 'And then [person] said...'"

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~80–120 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 3: REFUSAL / DOUBT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create tension through hesitation. The viewer
thinks "I'd be scared too." This makes the eventual courage
feel earned, not easy.

Content requirement: Show the protagonist's resistance. Why
didn't they just... do the thing? What fear, excuse, or
practical obstacle held them back? Make it relatable.

Mandatory field: A specific excuse or fear the viewer has
also felt. ("I told myself [specific rationalization]." or
"I was afraid that [specific outcome].")

Prompt instruction: "Show the hesitation. The protagonist
doesn't immediately leap. They [specific avoidance behavior].
They tell themselves [specific excuse]. Include the internal
argument: the part that wants to change vs. the part that's
terrified. Make the viewer recognize their own resistance."

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~80–100 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 4: MEETING THE MENTOR / DISCOVERING THE INSIGHT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create hope. A door opens. The viewer thinks
"maybe there IS a way."

Content requirement: Introduce the key knowledge, person,
resource, or epiphany that makes the journey possible. Not
a generic "I found a course." A specific moment of understanding.

Mandatory field: The insight stated in one clean sentence.
What did the mentor/book/experience/failure teach that changed
the protagonist's approach?

Prompt instruction: "Introduce the turning point. Not 'I found
a solution.' What was the SPECIFIC insight? Who delivered it,
or what experience forced it? State the insight in one sentence.
Then show the protagonist's reaction: the moment it clicked.
'And suddenly [old belief] didn't make sense anymore.'"

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~100–130 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 5: THE ORDEAL
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create the emotional low point. The viewer feels
the weight of the struggle. This is the darkest moment before
the breakthrough — and it makes the victory MEAN something.

Content requirement: The central struggle. The hardest lesson.
The moment of failure, loss, or breaking. A specific scene,
not a vague "it was hard."

Mandatory field: A concrete scene of failure or crisis. Where
were they? What happened? What did they feel in their body?
One sentence of internal monologue at the lowest point.

Prompt instruction: "Write the hardest moment as a SCENE.
Specific time, specific place, specific sensory details. What
went wrong? What did it cost? Include the internal monologue
at the breaking point: the thought that almost made them quit.
'I was [place], [time], and I thought: [specific thought].'
Make the viewer feel the weight."

Length: [2 paragraphs / ~120–150 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 6: THE REWARD / TRANSFORMATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create relief and triumph. The breakthrough lands.
The viewer feels the release of tension.

Content requirement: The moment of breakthrough. What changed?
What new capability, perspective, or understanding emerged
FROM the ordeal? The transformation must be clearly caused
by the struggle, not despite it.

Mandatory field: A before/after contrast in one sentence.
"Before the ordeal, I [old behavior/belief]. After, I [new
behavior/belief]."

Prompt instruction: "Write the breakthrough. Not 'eventually
things got better.' The SPECIFIC moment or realization that
marked the shift. What did the ordeal TEACH that nothing else
could? Show the new capability in action: a specific scene
where the protagonist does something they couldn't do before."

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~100–130 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 7: RETURN WITH THE ELIXIR
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create universality and actionability. The viewer
thinks "I can do this too." The personal story becomes THEIR
roadmap.

Content requirement: Bring the lesson back to the viewer's
ordinary world (Beat 1). Make it actionable. What can THEY
do with this insight? Tie back explicitly to the "before"
state from Beat 1.

Mandatory field: A direct callback to a specific detail from
Beat 1, now recontextualized. AND one concrete action the
viewer can take today.

Prompt instruction: "Return to the viewer. 'You're probably
[Beat 1 behavior] right now. Here's what I'd tell you...'
Give ONE specific, actionable step. Then close the loop:
reference the Beat 1 detail and show how it looks different
now. End with a statement of what's possible. Not 'success.'
Something specific and felt."

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~100–130 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GUARDRAIL STACK
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[PASTE FROM TOOL 5]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OUTPUT FORMAT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Write with [BEAT 1: ORDINARY WORLD], [BEAT 2: CALL], etc. headers.
- After the skeleton, add "Arc Verification":
  1. "Identification point (Beat 1): [the specific detail]"
  2. "Lowest point (Beat 5): [the specific scene]"
  3. "Callback in Beat 7: [the Beat 1 detail, transformed]"
- No style. No jokes. No pacing. Pure structure.
```
