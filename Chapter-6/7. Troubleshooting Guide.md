## 7. Troubleshooting Guide

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| The script reads like a textbook; emotional beats feel flat. | Guardrails were too weak or not included. | Strengthen the Concreteness and Emotional Spike guards. Add explicit examples of the desired language (e.g., “show the viewer’s clenched jaw, the half‑drunk coffee, the glare of the screen at 2 a.m.”). |
| Two beats merged into one; a required beat is missing. | Prompt too long, or the AI simplified the structure. | Split the prompt: send beats 1–3, then continue with the remaining beats in a follow‑up prompt, using the previous output as context. |
| The final beat doesn’t reference the opening; script feels incomplete. | Payoff‑Reference Guard not strong enough. | Manually add the callback in the prompt: “The final beat MUST explicitly mention [specific detail from Beat 1], e.g., ‘That same coffee stain on the desk now reminds me…’” |
| The AI still starts a section with “In this section…” | Summary Prohibition Guard missing or ignored. | Move the guard to the very beginning of the prompt in bold: “UNDER NO CIRCUMSTANCES begin any section with meta‑commentary like ‘In this section…’ or ‘To summarize…’.” |
| Agitation feels mild, not escalating. | Stake‑Escalation Guard not effectively enforced. | Add a concrete check: “After Beat 3, I should feel a knot in my stomach. If the text doesn’t achieve that, rewrite it.” |
