## 5. Guardrail Stack Library

*Copy and paste the relevant guardrails into your structural prompt to prevent summary mode. Select three for a strong defense; add more for longer or complex scripts.*

**Universal Guardrails (Copy‑Paste Ready):**

**The Concreteness Guard:**  
“At every beat, you must include at least one unexpected, specific sensory detail—a color, a sound, a number, a physical sensation—that makes the scene feel real and lived-in. Never use abstract nouns without a concrete anchor.”

**The Emotional Spike Guard:**  
“At Beats 2, 3, and 5, you must include a sentence that explicitly triggers an emotional response—use words or scenarios that would make a viewer feel indignation, relief, fear, or humor. After each of these sentences, add a brief note in parentheses explaining which emotion you aimed for.”

**The Summary Prohibition Guard:**  
“You must never begin a beat with ‘In this section, we will…’ or ‘To summarize…’ or any meta‑commentary about the script structure. Every sentence must be in the direct narrative voice of the script, not a description of the script.”

**The Stake‑Escalation Guard:**  
“Between each Agitation layer (or each beat from Ordinary World to Ordeal), the stakes must explicitly increase. After each beat, I will check that the viewer has more to lose than they did in the previous beat. If they don’t, rewrite until they do.”

**The Payoff‑Reference Guard:**  
“The final beat must include a specific callback—a phrase, a setting, or a sensory detail—that appeared in the first beat. This loop closure is mandatory; without it, the script is incomplete.”
