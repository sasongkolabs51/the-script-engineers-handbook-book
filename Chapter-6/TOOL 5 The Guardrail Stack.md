## TOOL 5: The Guardrail Stack

### *Paste at the end of EVERY framework prompt. Prevents summary mode. Forces emotional specificity. Non-negotiable.*

---

### The Five Universal Guardrails

Copy the ones relevant to your framework. For most scripts, use at least three.

---

**GUARDRAIL 1: The Concreteness Guard**

```
CONCRETENESS GUARD: At every beat, you must include at least one
unexpected, specific sensory detail — a color, a sound, a number,
a physical sensation, a brand name, a timestamp — that makes the
scene feel real and lived-in. Never use abstract nouns ("success,"
"frustration," "growth") without immediately anchoring them to a
concrete image. Not "I felt frustrated" but "I was staring at the
blinking cursor at 1:47 a.m., cold coffee in a mug that said
'World's Okayest Designer.'" If a sentence could appear in any
generic self-help article, rewrite it until it couldn't.
```

---

**GUARDRAIL 2: The Emotional Spike Guard**

```
EMOTIONAL SPIKE GUARD: At Beats [2, 3, and 5 / adjust per
framework], you must include a sentence explicitly designed to
trigger a specific emotional response — indignation, relief,
fear, recognition, dark humor. After each of these sentences,
add a brief parenthetical note: (Emotion target: [name]). This
forces you to monitor emotional trajectory. If two consecutive
beats target the same emotion, adjust one. The arc must VARY:
recognition → discomfort → concern → hope → empowerment.
```

---

**GUARDRAIL 3: The Summary Prohibition Guard**

```
SUMMARY PROHIBITION GUARD: You must NEVER begin a beat with
"In this section," "To summarize," "As I mentioned," "Let's
move on to," or any meta-commentary about the script's structure.
Every sentence must be in the DIRECT NARRATIVE VOICE of the
script. You are not describing what the script will do. You ARE
the script. If you catch yourself writing "Now we'll explore..."
delete it and write the exploration itself.
```

---

**GUARDRAIL 4: The Stake-Escalation Guard**

```
STAKE-ESCALATION GUARD: Between each consecutive beat, the stakes
must EXPLICITLY increase. After each beat, verify: does the viewer
have MORE to lose than they did in the previous beat? If not,
rewrite until they do. The escalation can be: temporal (daily
cost → yearly cost → lifetime cost), relational (personal →
professional → identity), or emotional (irritation → fear →
existential threat). Mark the escalation: each beat should feel
like the temperature rising one degree.
```

---

**GUARDRAIL 5: The Payoff-Reference Guard**

```
PAYOFF-REFERENCE GUARD: The FINAL beat must include a specific
callback — a phrase, a setting, a sensory detail, a number —
that appeared in the FIRST beat. This loop closure is MANDATORY.
Without it, the script is structurally incomplete. The callback
should show transformation: the same detail, now changed. (The
cold coffee is now warm. The 2 a.m. timestamp is now 10 p.m.
The blinking cursor is now a completed document.) If the final
beat does not reference the first beat, the script fails.
```

### Niche-Specific Guardrail Add-Ons (Optional)

Add these to your stack based on your content type:

| Niche | Additional Guardrail |
|---|---|
| **Tech reviews** | "Never explain a spec without immediately translating it into a user-experience consequence. Not '16GB RAM' but 'you can keep 47 Chrome tabs open without the fan sounding like a jet engine.'" |
| **Personal finance** | "Every dollar amount must be contextualized: what does that money MEAN in the viewer's life? Not '$500/month' but 'that's the vacation you keep postponing.'" |
| **Health/fitness** | "Never use generic body language ('feel better,' 'get fit'). Use specific physical sensations: 'the feeling of climbing stairs without your thighs burning' or 'sleeping through the night without waking at 3 a.m.'" |
| **Education/how-to** | "Every concept must be immediately followed by a concrete example the viewer can replicate in the next 10 minutes. No concept floats without an anchor." |
| **Business/career** | "Every business insight must include a specific, named scenario: a company, a role, a conversation. Not 'companies value initiative' but 'the hiring manager at [type of company] who told me...'" |
