## 3. Guardrail Stack Template

**Purpose:** A set of reusable prompt insertions that prevent the AI from slipping into summary mode. Paste this entire block (or selected guardrails) at the end of your Layer 2 skeleton prompt, after the beat script.

```
GUARDRAIL STACK (apply throughout the entire script):

1. CONCRETENESS GUARD: At every beat, you must include at least one unexpected, specific sensory detail—a color, a sound, a number, a physical sensation—that makes the scene feel real and lived‑in. Never use abstract nouns without a concrete anchor.

2. EMOTIONAL SPIKE GUARD: At beats that call for emotional escalation (e.g., Agitation layers, Ordeal), you must include a sentence that is designed to trigger a distinct emotional response—indignation, relief, fear, humour. After such a sentence, add a short note in parentheses noting the intended emotion: (emotion: indignation).

3. SUMMARY PROHIBITION GUARD: You must never begin a section with “In this section, we will…” or “To summarize…” or any meta‑commentary about the script’s structure. Every sentence must be in the direct narrative voice of the script, not a description of it.

4. STAKE‑ESCALATION GUARD: Between consecutive beats that move the viewer deeper into the problem (e.g., Agitation 1→2, Ordinary World→Ordeal), the stakes must explicitly increase. After each such beat, I will check: does the viewer now have more to lose than in the previous beat? If not, rewrite.

5. PAYOFF‑REFERENCE GUARD: The final beat (Empowered Future, Return with the Elixir, or Offer) must include a specific callback—a phrase, setting, or sensory detail—that appeared in the first beat. This loop closure is mandatory; without it, the script is incomplete.
```

**Usage:** For each video, you can activate all five, or select the ones most relevant. For example, in a short Hook‑Story‑Offer video you might only need Concreteness, Summary Prohibition, and Payoff‑Reference.
