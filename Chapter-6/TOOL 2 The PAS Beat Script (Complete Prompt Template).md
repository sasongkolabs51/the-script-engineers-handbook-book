## TOOL 2: The PAS Beat Script (Complete Prompt Template)

### *Your first framework module. Fill in the brackets. Run as your Layer 2 structural prompt. Six beats, each with a mandatory emotional job.*

---

```
═══ FRAMEWORK: PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution) ═══
═══ STRUCTURAL SKELETON PROMPT ═══

TOPIC: [your specific topic]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [who's watching — age, struggle, what they've tried]
THE SPECIFIC PROBLEM: [not "productivity" but "the 3-week app
abandonment cycle"]
MY SOLUTION/INSIGHT: [the core reframe or method — one sentence]
VIDEO LENGTH TARGET: [___ minutes / ~___ words]

Write a complete script skeleton following this EXACT beat sequence.
Each beat has a mandatory emotional job and content requirement.
Do NOT skip beats. Do NOT merge beats. Do NOT add summary sentences
between beats. Execute each beat as specified.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 1: PROBLEM IDENTIFICATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create instant recognition. The viewer must feel
personally seen within the first sentence.

Content requirement: Describe a SPECIFIC moment from the viewer's
daily life that they instantly recognize as the problem. Use a
sensory detail — a time of day, a physical sensation, a thought
they've had verbatim, an object on their desk.

Mandatory field: Include ONE concrete detail (number, quote,
specific object, exact time) that could not apply to any other
video on this topic.

Prompt instruction: "Describe the moment the viewer realizes they
have this problem. Not abstractly. Specifically. They're [doing X]
at [time/place] and they feel [specific emotion]. Include the
thought that crosses their mind verbatim."

Length: [2–3 paragraphs / ~100–150 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 2: AGITATION LAYER 1 — IMMEDIATE CONSEQUENCES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Transform recognition into discomfort. The viewer
should feel the daily irritation of living with this problem.

Content requirement: Show the immediate, tangible cost. Wasted
time. Repeated mistakes. Small daily frictions that accumulate.
Use a concrete scenario, not abstract language.

Mandatory field: A quantifiable cost or a specific repeated
scenario. ("Every morning, you lose 23 minutes to..." or "Three
times this week, you've...")

Prompt instruction: "Show the viewer losing something specific
TODAY. Not 'in general.' Today. This morning. This afternoon.
What did this problem cost them in the last 24 hours? Make it
tangible: minutes, dollars, a specific conversation that went
wrong."

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~80–120 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 3: AGITATION LAYER 2 — ESCALATED CONSEQUENCES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Transform discomfort into genuine concern. The
viewer should feel the long-term stakes.

Content requirement: Project forward. If this problem persists
for 6–12 months, what specifically will the viewer have lost?
Expand to larger life domains: career, relationships, identity,
health.

Mandatory field: A forward-looking "you will have..." statement
that paints the future cost in vivid, personal terms.

Prompt instruction: "Project forward one year. The viewer hasn't
fixed this. Write a 'By next [month/season], you will have...'
paragraph that makes the cost feel real and personal. Not
'success will be harder.' Specific: 'You will have watched
three colleagues get promoted while you...' or 'You will have
deleted and re-downloaded the same app eleven more times.'"

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~80–120 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 4: AGITATION LAYER 3 — THE ROCK BOTTOM MOMENT (optional
for shorter videos; mandatory for 10+ min)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Make the problem feel existential. The viewer
should think "this is who I'm becoming if I don't change."

Content requirement: A vivid worst-case scenario OR a personal
confession of your own rock bottom with this problem. A specific
scene. A specific moment. Not "it was bad" but the exact texture
of the worst moment.

Mandatory field: A sensory-rich scene with a specific detail
that makes it feel real. (The cold coffee. The 2 a.m. timestamp.
The exact words someone said.)

Prompt instruction: "Write the worst moment. The moment the
viewer (or I) realized this wasn't just an inconvenience — it
was defining who they're becoming. Include a specific scene:
where they are, what they see, what they feel in their body.
One sentence of internal monologue."

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~80–100 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 5: SOLUTION INTRODUCTION — THE PIVOT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Shift from tension to hope. But EARN the shift.
Don't announce a solution. REVEAL it as the result of a specific
insight or discovery moment.

Content requirement: The conceptual hinge of the script. A single,
powerful insight that redefines the problem. Not a list of tips.
A reframe. A "what if the problem isn't X but Y?" moment.

Mandatory field: A sentence beginning with "I realized..." or
"The shift happened when..." or "And then I saw it—" that names
the core insight in one clean line.

Prompt instruction: "Pivot sharply. The tone changes here. From
heavy to light. From stuck to moving. Introduce the solution as
a DISCOVERY, not a prescription. 'I realized [insight].' Then
one sentence explaining why this reframe changes everything.
Do NOT list steps yet. Just the insight. Let it land."

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~80–100 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 6: SOLUTION EXPANSION — HOW IT WORKS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Build credibility and clarity. The viewer should
understand the MECHANISM, not just the promise.

Content requirement: Break the solution into 2–3 clear steps.
Each step has a concrete example from the niche. Use second
person ("you") and present-tense action language.

Mandatory field: At least ONE specific, repeatable action the
viewer can take TODAY, described in simple language. Not
"optimize your workflow" but "open your phone settings and
turn off notifications for these three specific apps."

Prompt instruction: "Explain the solution in 2–3 steps. Each
step: name it in 3–5 words, explain the mechanism in one
sentence, give a concrete example. Use 'you' language. Present
tense. The viewer should be able to DO step one within the
next hour."

Length: [2–3 paragraphs / ~150–200 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 7: EMPOWERED FUTURE — THE PAYOFF
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create a sense of attainable transformation. The
viewer should feel capable, motivated, and slightly excited.

Content requirement: Return to the EXACT scenario from Beat 1.
Same time of day. Same setting. Same object. But everything
feels different now. The viewer is living the "after" version.

Mandatory field: A direct callback to the sensory detail from
Beat 1, now transformed. (The cold coffee is now warm because
they finished the task in 20 minutes. The 2 a.m. screen glare
is now a 10 p.m. "I'm done" sigh of relief.)

Prompt instruction: "Rewrite the Beat 1 scene as it looks AFTER
the solution. Same setting, same time, same sensory details —
but the emotional texture is completely different. End with one
sentence naming what the viewer now HAS that they didn't before.
Not 'success.' Something specific: 'the ability to close your
laptop at 6 p.m. without guilt.'"

Length: [1–2 paragraphs / ~80–100 words]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GUARDRAIL STACK (paste from Tool 5)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[PASTE YOUR SELECTED GUARDRAILS HERE]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OUTPUT FORMAT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Write the full skeleton with [BEAT 1], [BEAT 2], etc. headers.
- Include approximate word counts per beat.
- After the skeleton, add a 3-line "Arc Verification":
  1. "Stakes at Beat 1: [describe]"
  2. "Stakes at Beat 4: [describe — must be HIGHER]"
  3. "Callback in Beat 7: [the specific detail that echoes Beat 1]"
- Do NOT add stylistic flourishes, jokes, or pacing markers.
  This is pure structure. Voice and pacing come in Layers 3–4.
```
