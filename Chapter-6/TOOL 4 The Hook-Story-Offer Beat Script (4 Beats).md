## TOOL 4: The Hook-Story-Offer Beat Script (4 Beats)

### *For conversion content, case studies, product recommendations, and "here's what worked" videos. Tight, emotionally primed, action-oriented.*

---

```
═══ FRAMEWORK: HOOK-STORY-OFFER (4 Beats) ═══
═══ STRUCTURAL SKELETON PROMPT ═══

TOPIC: [your specific topic]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [who's watching]
THE HOOK ARCHETYPE: [from Chapter 5: Curiosity Gap / Contrarian
Claim / Relatable Pain / Specific Promise / Identity Challenge /
Open Loop]
THE STORY: [the specific personal narrative or case study —
who, what, when, where, before/after]
THE INSIGHT: [the universal principle extracted from the story]
THE OFFER: [the specific action, product, resource, or perspective
shift you're recommending]
VIDEO LENGTH TARGET: [___ minutes / ~___ words]

Write a complete script skeleton following this EXACT 4-beat
sequence.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 1: HOOK (60–80 words)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Stop the scroll. Lock attention. Create an
unanswered question or emotional spike.

Content requirement: Use the Hook-Retention Architecture from
Chapter 5. Select the archetype specified above. Chain into
a retention mechanism (Open Loop Extension, Pattern Interrupt,
or Stake Escalation).

Mandatory field: One specific concrete detail (number, name,
sensory moment). A nameable emotion triggered in 3 seconds.
A forward-pointing retention lock.

Prompt instruction: [PASTE YOUR WINNING HOOK FROM CHAPTER 5
TOOLKIT, OR USE THE HOOK GENERATION MACHINE PROMPT]

Length: [60–80 words / 4–6 sentences]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 2: STORY (200–350 words)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Immerse the viewer in a narrative. Create
emotional proof that the insight is real. The viewer should
feel they're LIVING the experience alongside the protagonist.

Content requirement: Tell the specific story. A real person
(you or a case study). A timeline ("over three months"). A
clear before-and-after contrast. Sensory details. A low point.
A turning point.

Mandatory fields:
- A specific person (name or "I")
- A specific timeline ("In [month], I..." or "Over [X weeks]...")
- A before-state and after-state clearly contrasted
- One low-point moment (sensory scene)
- One turning-point moment (the insight arrives)

Prompt instruction: "Tell the story as a NARRATIVE, not a
summary. Scene by scene. Start in the 'before.' Show the
struggle. Hit the low point. Then the turn. Use dialogue
if natural. Use sensory details. The viewer should feel
time passing. Include at least one moment of doubt and one
moment of breakthrough. End the story at the moment of
transformation — don't explain the lesson yet."

Length: [200–350 words / 4–6 short paragraphs]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 3: INSIGHT / PRINCIPLE (80–120 words)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create the "aha" moment. The viewer understands
WHY the story worked. The universal principle clicks.

Content requirement: Extract the lesson from the story. State
it cleanly in one sentence. Then explain the mechanism in
2–3 sentences. Why does this principle work? What does it
reframe?

Mandatory field: The principle stated in ONE sentence that
could be written on a sticky note. Then 2–3 sentences of
"here's why this works" explanation.

Prompt instruction: "Pull back from the story. 'Here's what
that experience taught me: [one-sentence principle].' Then
explain the mechanism: why does this work? What does it
reframe about the viewer's assumptions? Keep it tight. No
rambling. The insight should feel like a key turning in a
lock."

Length: [80–120 words / 2 paragraphs]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
BEAT 4: OFFER / CTA (60–100 words)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emotional job: Create a natural, low-pressure invitation to
act. The viewer should feel that the next step is the LOGICAL
continuation of the insight, not a sales pitch.

Content requirement: Introduce the specific action, product,
resource, or perspective shift. Frame it as the viewer's
next step in THEIR story. Reference the pain from the hook.
Reference the possibility from the story.

Mandatory fields:
- A callback to the hook's pain point or question
- The specific offer stated clearly (what it is, what it does)
- One sentence framing the action as the viewer's own next step
- Low-pressure language. An invitation, not a command.

Prompt instruction: "Connect the offer to the hook: 'If you're
still [pain from Beat 1]...' Then introduce the offer as the
logical next step: 'The thing that changed everything for me
was [offer]. It's [what it is]. And if you want [outcome from
the story], it's [where to find it].' End with an invitation,
not a command. 'If that resonates, [specific action]. If not,
no pressure — but I'd hate for you to spend another [timeframe]
stuck in [Beat 1 pain].'"

Length: [60–100 words / 2 paragraphs]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GUARDRAIL STACK
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
[PASTE FROM TOOL 5]

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OUTPUT FORMAT
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Write with [BEAT 1: HOOK], [BEAT 2: STORY], etc. headers.
- After the skeleton, add "Arc Verification":
  1. "Hook question/pain: [what was opened]"
  2. "Story low point: [the specific scene]"
  3. "Offer callback: [how the CTA references the hook]"
- No style. No pacing. Pure structure.
```
