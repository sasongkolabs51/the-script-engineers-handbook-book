### Upgraded Layer 1: Ideation + Framework Selection

```
═══ LAYER 1: IDEATION (UPGRADED WITH FRAMEWORK) ═══

I am creating a [platform] video for [audience].

TOPIC: [your topic]
ANGLE: [your specific take]
EMOTIONAL OUTCOME: [what the viewer should feel by the end]
PERSONAL ELEMENT: [your story / data / vulnerability]

NARRATIVE FRAMEWORK: [PAS / Hero's Journey / Hook-Story-Offer /
other: ___]

FRAMEWORK BEAT SCRIPT:
[PASTE YOUR COMPLETED BEAT SCRIPT FROM TOOL 2, 3, 4, OR 6]

Generate a structured beat sheet that:
1. Follows the framework beat sequence EXACTLY.
2. Populates each beat with 2–3 sentences of specific content
   relevant to my topic and angle.
3. Includes the mandatory field for each beat (the specific
   detail, number, scene, or callback).
4. Notes the emotional temperature for each beat.
5. Identifies where the hook (from Chapter 5) will sit
   (Beat 1 for PAS; Beat 1 for HSO; Beat 1–2 for Hero's Journey).

Do NOT write prose. Do NOT add tone or style. Only the structural
blueprint with content specifics per beat.
```

### Upgraded Layer 2: Structural Skeleton with Framework + Guardrails

```
═══ LAYER 2: STRUCTURAL SKELETON (WITH FRAMEWORK + GUARDRAILS) ═══

Using the following beat sheet, write a complete script skeleton.

BEAT SHEET:
"""
[PASTE YOUR APPROVED LAYER 1 BEAT SHEET HERE]
"""

"""
[PASTE YOUR WINNING HOOK FROM CHAPTER 5 HERE]

- Follow the beat sequence EXACTLY. One section per beat.
- Each beat must fulfill its emotional job and include its

  rhythm. No personality. Pure structure and logic.
- Include [HOOK] at the opening (already provided above).
- Include [TENSION LOOP] where the framework creates an
  unanswered question.
- Include [CTA] at the closing position.
- Include [SPECIFIC DETAIL NEEDED: ___] where concrete examples
  should go but aren't yet specified.

GUARDRAIL STACK:
"""
[PASTE YOUR SELECTED GUARDRAILS FROM TOOL 5]
"""

OUTPUT FORMAT:
- Write with [BEAT 1: NAME], [BEAT 2: NAME], etc. headers.
- After the skeleton, add "Arc Verification":
  1. Emotional arc: [list the emotion per beat]
  2. Stakes escalation: [confirm each beat raises stakes]
  3. Callback: [the Beat 1 detail echoed in the final beat]
  4. Hook integration: [confirm the hook flows into Beat 1/2]
```

### Layers 3 and 4: No Changes Needed

Your pacing injection (Layer 3) and voice overlay (Layer 4) prompts from Chapter 4 work exactly as before. The framework is embedded in the skeleton. Pacing and voice are applied on top. The system is modular.

**One addition for Layer 3:** You can now add framework-aware pacing guidance:

```
FRAMEWORK-AWARE PACING GUIDANCE:
- Agitation / Ordeal beats: [SLOW DOWN], longer sentences,
  heavier emotional weight. Let the discomfort breathe.
- Solution / Transformation beats: [FAST ENERGY] on the pivot
  sentence, then [SLOW DOWN] for the explanation. The shift
  should feel like a door opening.
- Hook beat: [FAST ENERGY] or sharp delivery. No [PAUSE] before
  the hook — momentum is everything.
- Final beat / Payoff: [SLOW DOWN], warm energy. One [PAUSE 2s]
  before the callback line. Let the resolution land.
```
