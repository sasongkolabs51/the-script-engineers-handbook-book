## TOOL 3: Platform Adaptation Blueprints

### *Extend the system beyond YouTube. Same principles. Different parameters. One system, every container.*

---

### How to Use These

Each platform has a consumption context that shapes how hooks land, how pacing works, and how CTAs convert. These blueprints are *starting points*—add them as new columns in your Niche Adaptation Blueprint (Chapter 9, Tool 2). Refine them with your own data through the Evolution Loop.

---

### PLATFORM: TikTok / Short-Form Vertical (<60 sec)

| Parameter | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Must land in under 1.5 seconds. First FRAME is the hook. Visual + verbal simultaneously. Use Pattern Interrupt or Contrarian Claim. No preamble. No greeting. Drop mid-thought. |
| **Structure** | Compressed to 2–3 beats max. Hook → One insight/payoff → CTA (often simultaneous). No time for multi-layer agitation. |
| **Pacing** | [Fast energy] throughout. [Hard cut] between beats. [Punch] on the key word. [Text on screen] for every key point (viewers watch without sound). Max 1 [Pause 0.5s]. No [Pause 3s]. No [Slow down]. |
| **Voice** | Compress humor density. Keep Colloquial Fingerprint. Shorter sentences (max 10 words). More fragments. Higher energy baseline. |
| **CTA** | Woven into the final line. Not a separate section. Identity Reinforcement or Curiosity Bridge. "Follow for part 2" or "If you're the kind of person who [X], you need this." Max 5 seconds. |
| **B-Roll** | Constant visual change. Cut every 2–3 seconds. Text overlays. Reaction cam. Source clips. No static talking head for more than 3 seconds. |
| **Session Flow** | Loop the ending back to the beginning (TikTok replays). The last sentence should connect to the first, creating an infinite loop. |
| **Guardrails** | "Never exceed 60 seconds." "Every sentence must be understandable without audio (text on screen)." "No sentence over 10 words." "The hook IS the first frame—no setup." |

### PLATFORM: Instagram Reels (15–90 sec)

| Parameter | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Visual-first. The first frame must stop the scroll even on mute. Text overlay with the hook claim. Verbal hook lands in 2 seconds. |
| **Structure** | 2–4 beats. Hook → Demonstration/Story → Payoff → CTA. Slightly more room than TikTok for a mini-narrative. |
| **Pacing** | Fast but rhythmic. [Fast energy] with [Beat] tokens for comedic timing. [Text on screen] for key points. Visual rhythm matters more than verbal rhythm. |
| **Voice** | Slightly more polished than TikTok. Keep fragments. Keep signature phrases. Energy: confident, slightly performative (Reels rewards "presenter energy" more than TikTok). |
| **CTA** | "Save this for later" or "Send this to someone who needs it." Share/save CTAs outperform subscribe CTAs on Reels. Identity Reinforcement works well. |
| **B-Roll** | Aesthetic. Clean visuals. Text overlays with consistent font/color. Product close-ups. Before/after splits. |
| **Guardrails** | "Must work on mute." "Text on screen for every key claim." "Visual change every 3–4 seconds." "CTA is a save/share trigger, not a follow beg." |

### PLATFORM: LinkedIn Video (1–5 min)

| Parameter | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Contrarian Claim about industry norms. Or Specific Promise with a professional outcome. Slightly more formal energy than YouTube, but NOT corporate. Authority through precision. |
| **Structure** | Hook-Story-Offer works well. Story = professional case study. 3–5 beats. More room for evidence and data than TikTok. |
| **Pacing** | Moderate. Authoritative. [Slow emphasis] on key data points. [Text on screen] for statistics. [Pause 1.5s] after a contrarian claim. No [Fast energy] (too casual). No [Hard cut] (too jarring). |
| **Voice** | Shift emotional temperature 10% cooler. More analytical. Less self-deprecation (save it for YouTube). Maintain Colloquial Fingerprint but reduce slang. Contractions still fine. |
| **CTA** | Empowered Utility or Social Proof. "If this resonates, share it with a colleague who's dealing with [X]." Professional sharing > subscribing. |
| **B-Roll** | Data slides. Article screenshots. Professional environment shots. Clean, minimal. No memes. No reaction cam. |
| **Guardrails** | "Never use slang or internet-speak." "Every claim backed by a specific number or case study." "Tone: expert colleague, not entertainer." "CTA is a share/engage trigger, not a subscribe beg." |

### PLATFORM: Live Streaming / Unscripted Segments

| Parameter | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Prepare 3 hook options before going live. Deploy conversationally. Don't read—speak. Use your Voice Profile as the anchor. |
| **Structure** | Modular segment templates. Prepare 4–6 "segment cards" (each with a hook, 2–3 talking points, and a transition). Deploy in order or adapt based on chat energy. |
| **Pacing** | Conversational. No tokens (you're live). But prepare [PAUSE] moments in your segment cards: "After making this point, stop. Let chat react. 3 seconds of silence." |
| **Voice** | Full baseline. No modifications. Live is where your unscripted voice shines. Trust your Colloquial Fingerprint. |
| **CTA** | Woven into natural transitions. "If you're just joining, here's what we're talking about..." Mid-stream reminders, not end-of-stream begs. |
| **Preparation** | Write segment cards, not a script. Each card: one hook sentence, 3 bullet points, one transition sentence. That's it. The rest is you, live, in your voice. |
| **Guardrails** | "Never read a script on stream." "Segment cards are prompts, not teleprompters." "If chat energy shifts, abandon the card order." "Your Voice Profile is the anchor—speak like you, not like a presenter." |
