## TOOL 9: Hook Troubleshooting Guide

### *When the hook isn't landing, diagnose it here. Specific symptoms, specific fixes.*

---

| Symptom | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Hook feels generic — could be any video, any creator | **Specificity failure.** No concrete detail. | Add to prompt: "Include a specific number / date / location / sensory detail that makes this hook feel uniquely personal. Not 'many people' but '43 apps.' Not 'a while ago' but 'Tuesday, 2:15 p.m.'" |
| Hook is interesting but viewer could still walk away | **Curiosity gap too small.** The question isn't urgent enough. | Strengthen the gap: "The fact you present must be INCOMPLETE. The viewer must feel they are missing a crucial piece. Add: 'And the reason has nothing to do with [obvious answer]' to deepen the mystery." |
| Hook grabs attention but the next sentence drops energy | **Retention chain broken.** Transition is a summary, not an escalation. | Add to prompt: "The sentence immediately after the hook must NOT summarize, explain, or transition. It must DEEPEN the mystery, BREAK an expectation, or RAISE the stakes. No 'In this video...' No 'Let me explain.'" |
| Hook feels clickbaity / manipulative / dishonest | **Authenticity gap.** The claim isn't grounded in real experience. | Add: "The hook must feel like a genuine insight earned through experience, not a provocation for clicks. Ground it: 'After [X time/effort], I learned...' The viewer should trust that the payoff will deliver." |
| Hook is strong but doesn't sound like me | **Voice not specified in hook prompt.** | Add your Voice DNA elements to the hook prompt: sentence length, vocabulary, humor style. Or generate the hook structurally, then apply voice in Layer 4. |
| AI ignores the retention mechanism instruction | **Mechanism described too abstractly.** | Replace abstract description with a concrete example: "For the Pattern Interrupt, do this: Hook says [X]. Next sentence says 'But it wasn't [X]. It was [Y].' The pivot must be surprising. Example: 'I deleted every app. Not because I found a better one—but because the apps were never the problem.'" |
| Hook is too long (>80 words, >15 seconds) | **Overwritten.** Too much setup before the punch. | Add: "Maximum 60 words. Maximum 4 sentences. The hook must land in the FIRST sentence. No preamble. No context. No 'So I've been thinking about...' Drop the viewer in mid-thought." |
| All three generated hooks sound the same | **Prompt too constrained / archetype too narrow.** | Vary the structural approach: "Variation A: lead with the number. Variation B: lead with the personal moment. Variation C: lead with the contradiction." Force different entry points. |
| Hook works on paper but falls flat when read aloud | **Rhythm not considered.** Written hooks ≠ spoken hooks. | Read it aloud. Mark where you stumble. Add to prompt: "Write for the EAR, not the eye. Vary sentence length. Include one fragment. The hook should sound natural when spoken at conversational speed." |
| I keep defaulting to the same archetype every time | **Comfort zone.** You're not exercising range. | Force yourself to use a different archetype for the next 3 videos. Even if it feels unnatural. The Scroll-Stop Scorecard will tell you if it works. Range is a skill. |

---

### The Emergency Hook Fix (When You're 30 Minutes from Recording)

If you're in a rush and the hook isn't working, do this:

1. **Cut the first sentence.** Whatever it is. Delete it. Start with the second sentence. (The first sentence is almost always preamble.)
2. **Add a number.** Any specific number. "43 apps." "$4,300." "Day 17." "3 p.m." Numbers create instant specificity.
3. **End mid-thought.** Cut the last sentence of your hook in half. Let it hang. The viewer's brain will demand closure.
4. **Read it aloud once.** If you stumble, rewrite the stumble point. If you're bored, it's not ready.
