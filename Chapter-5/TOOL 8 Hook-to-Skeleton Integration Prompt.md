### *Feed your winning hook into Layer 2 so the entire skeleton inherits the hook's energy and retention architecture.*

---

```
═══ LAYER 2: STRUCTURAL SKELETON (with engineered hook) ═══

Using the following ENGINEERED HOOK as the opening, and this BEAT
SHEET as the structural blueprint, write a complete script skeleton.

ENGINEERED HOOK (use this as the first 60–80 words, verbatim or
with minimal adjustment):
"""
[PASTE YOUR WINNING HOOK + RETENTION LOCK FROM TOOL 3/6 HERE]
"""

BEAT SHEET:
"""
[PASTE YOUR APPROVED LAYER 1 BEAT SHEET HERE]
"""

INSTRUCTIONS:
- The script MUST open with the engineered hook above. Do not
  rewrite it. Do not soften it. Do not add a greeting before it.
- After the hook, the first structural beat must FLOW naturally
  from the retention mechanism. If the hook ended with an open
  loop, the first beat should provide CONTEXT for that loop
  (not resolve it). If the hook ended with a pattern interrupt,
  the first beat should explore the unexpected direction.
- The open loop / curiosity gap / tension established in the
  hook must remain UNRESOLVED until the designated payoff beat.
  Signpost it throughout: "Remember that [hook detail]? We'll
  get there."
- Each beat becomes 1–3 paragraphs of clear, functional prose.
- No stylistic flourishes. No jokes. No rhythm markers. Pure
  structure and logic.
- Include [HOOK] marker at the opening (already filled).
- Include [TENSION LOOP] markers where the hook's question is
  referenced but not answered.
- Include [CTA] placeholder at the closing position.
- Include [SPECIFIC DETAIL NEEDED: ___] where concrete examples
  should go.
- Total length: approximately [___] words.

The skeleton should maintain the hook's energy and forward
momentum throughout. The viewer who was locked in by the hook
should feel that momentum sustained — never dropped — until
the final payoff.
```
