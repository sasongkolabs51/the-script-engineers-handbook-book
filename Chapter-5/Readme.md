# Chapter 5 Companion Toolkit

## *The Hook Engineering Pack — From "Hey Everyone, Welcome Back" to Scroll-Stopping Precision*

---

## How to Use This Toolkit

Your hook is the only moment guaranteed to reach every viewer. Everything else—the structure, the pacing, the voice, the payoff—depends entirely on whether they stay. And “staying” gets decided in the first three to five seconds. That tiny window shapes everything that follows.

The tools in this kit help you transform your opening into a precisely engineered retention lock. You will choose an archetype that matches the emotional goal of your video, link it to a retention mechanism, prompt the AI to build it, and then confirm the result before you ever press record.

**Suggested order:**

- **Tool 1** (Archetype Selection Guide) — Begin here. Decide which emotional mechanism fits your video’s goal before you write a single word.
- **Tool 2** (The Six Archetype Prompt Templates) — Your core prompt library. One template per archetype. Fill in the brackets, generate, and evaluate.
- **Tool 3** (Hook-Loop Prompt Builder) — Connect your hook to a retention loop. This step locks attention for the first 15 seconds.
- **Tool 4** (The Scroll-Stop Scorecard) — Your quality checkpoint. Score every hook before you move forward.
- **Tool 5** (The 15-Second Retention Blueprint) — Map out your entire opening sequence, beat by beat.
- **Tool 6** (Hook Generation Machine) — The reusable all-in-one prompt for your layered workflow.
- **Tool 7** (Before/After Rewrite Worksheet) — Practice turning weak hooks into engineered ones. Build the instinct through repetition.
- **Tool 8** (Hook-to-Skeleton Integration Prompt) — Feed your winning hook smoothly into Layer 2.
- **Tool 9** (Troubleshooting Guide) — Diagnose and fix hooks that aren’t landing.
- **Tool 10** (30-Day Hook Drill Plan) — Develop fluency across all six archetypes with deliberate daily practice.

**The one rule:** Never write a hook prompt that says “make it engaging” or “grab attention.” Those words are wishes, not instructions. You are thinking like an engineer here. Specify the exact mechanism. The AI executes. You validate. That sequence is the entire workflow.
