## 5. AI Prompt Templates

### 5.1 Hook Generation Machine (Reusable)

This is your go‑to prompt for every new video. Fill in the bracketed parts, paste, and receive three engineered hooks scored with the Scroll‑Stop criteria.

```
I need a YouTube video hook for the following:
- Topic: [your topic]
- Target audience: [who you’re speaking to]
- Emotional goal: [e.g., make them feel empowered, challenge their assumptions, make them feel understood]

First, select the most appropriate hook archetype from this list: Curiosity Gap, Contrarian Claim, Relatable Pain, Specific Promise, Identity Challenge, Open Loop. Then generate a hook using the corresponding instruction below:
- Curiosity Gap: Open with a specific, unexpected fact or number that contradicts a common belief in my niche. Immediately state that the explanation will come later, but do not reveal it yet. Make the viewer feel they are missing something crucial.
- Contrarian Claim: Open with a blunt, confident statement that rejects a widely accepted best practice, popular tool, or common opinion in my niche. Make it sound like a hard‑won insight, not a hot take. Avoid softening phrases. The claim should feel slightly risky but genuine.
- Relatable Pain: Open with a detailed, almost uncomfortably specific description of a frustrating situation my ideal viewer has lived through. Use sensory details and precise language. Do not name the solution yet; sit in the pain for a moment.
- Specific Promise: Open with a direct statement of exactly what the viewer will be able to do, understand, or avoid after watching. The promise must be specific and measurable, anchored with a number or concrete capability.
- Identity Challenge: Open by naming a positive identity my target viewer holds about themselves and immediately suggest that a routine habit is silently working against that identity. Create a gap between who they think they are and what they’re actually doing.
- Open Loop: Open with the beginning of a specific personal story, scenario, or step‑by‑step process—and stop abruptly at the moment of highest tension or uncertainty. Do not provide any resolution. Signal that the resolution will arrive later in the video.

Immediately after the hook, chain in a retention mechanism. Choose the most fitting one:
- Open Loop Extension: Deepen the mystery with a tantalising detail that makes the unanswered question more urgent.
- Pattern Interrupt: Subvert the viewer’s prediction with a surprising pivot.
- Stake Escalation: Reveal a broader, more serious consequence that connects the hook to the viewer’s life.

Generate three distinct versions of the opening (hook + retention mechanism). After each, include a brief self‑assessment against the Scroll‑Stop Scorecard (Specificity, Emotional Spike, Curiosity Gap, Niche Authenticity, Retention Chain).
```

### 5.2 Hook‑to‑Skeleton Integration Prompt

Use this as your Layer 2 prompt after you’ve approved the beat sheet and hook.

```
Using the following hook:
[PASTE YOUR WINNING HOOK + RETENTION MECHANISM]

And this beat sheet:
[PASTE YOUR APPROVED BEAT SHEET FROM LAYER 1]

Write a complete script skeleton that begins with the hook and flows naturally into the first beat. Ensure:
- The structural skeleton maintains the retention chain established by the hook. If the hook created an open loop, keep that loop open throughout the skeleton until the designated payoff point. If it escalated stakes, keep raising them.
- Transitions between beats are smooth.
- [HOOK] placeholder is already filled; include [CTA] at the end.
- Prose is raw and functional—no stylistic flourishes yet.
```

### 5.3 Hook Debugging Prompt

When a hook fails the scorecard, paste it here along with the failing criteria to get a targeted fix.

```
The following hook scored low on the Scroll‑Stop Scorecard. The failing criteria are: [list criteria, e.g., Specificity, Curiosity Gap].

Hook:
[PASTE YOUR HOOK]

Please rewrite the hook to address exactly those weaknesses while preserving the original topic and angle. Then explain what you changed and why.
```
