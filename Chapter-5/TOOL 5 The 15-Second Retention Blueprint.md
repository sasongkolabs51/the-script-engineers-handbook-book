### The 15-Second Architecture

| Second | Beat | What Happens | Viewer's Psychological State | My Specific Content |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0–3 | **The Interrupt** | The hook lands. Pattern break. Thumb stops. | "Wait—what?" / "That's me." / "Hold on." | [Your hook sentence(s)] |
| 3–7 | **The Lock** | Retention mechanism activates. Mystery deepens / expectation breaks / stakes raise. | "I need to know more." / "That's not what I expected." / "Oh no, that's MY problem." | [Your retention lock sentence(s)] |
| 7–12 | **The Bridge** | Forward signal. The viewer is told (subtly) that the answer/resolution is coming. A micro-promise. | "Okay, I'll stick around for this." / "I'm invested now." | [Your forward-pointing sentence] |
| 12–15 | **The Handoff** | Transition into the body. The first structural beat begins. The viewer is now primed and oriented. | "Let's go." / "I'm here. Show me." | [First sentence of your body content] |

### The Anti-Pattern Check

**My opening does NOT:**

☐ Greet the audience ("Hey everyone, welcome back")
☐ State the topic directly ("Today we're talking about X")
☐ Summarize the video ("In this video, I'll cover A, B, and C")
☐ Ask a generic rhetorical question ("Have you ever wondered...?")
☐ Use filler before the hook ("So, um, I've been thinking about...")
☐ Bury the hook after 10 seconds of preamble

**My opening DOES:**

☐ Land the hook in the first sentence (second 0–1)
☐ Contain a specific, concrete detail (number, name, sensory moment)
☐ Trigger a nameable emotion within 3 seconds
☐ Chain into a retention mechanism by second 5
☐ Signal forward (the answer is coming) by second 10
☐ Hand off cleanly to the body by second 15
