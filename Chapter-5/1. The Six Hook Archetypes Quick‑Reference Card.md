## 1. The Six Hook Archetypes Quick‑Reference Card

*Keep this one‑pager next to your keyboard. When you need a hook, pick an archetype and use the corresponding prompt instruction.*

| **Archetype** | **What It Does** | **Prompt Instruction (Paste this into your prompt)** |
|---------------|------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| **Curiosity Gap** | Opens a knowledge hole the viewer needs to fill | *“Open with a specific, unexpected fact or number that contradicts a common belief in my niche. Immediately state that the explanation will come later in the video, but do not reveal it yet. Make the viewer feel they are missing something crucial.”* |
| **Contrarian Claim** | Directly challenges a widely accepted truth | *“Open with a blunt, confident statement that rejects a widely accepted best practice, popular tool, or common opinion in my niche. Make the claim sound like a hard‑won insight, not a hot take. Avoid softening phrases like ‘I think’ or ‘maybe.’ The claim should feel slightly risky but genuine.”* |
| **Relatable Pain** | Describes an unspoken frustration so vividly it feels personal | *“Open with a detailed, almost uncomfortably specific description of a frustrating situation my ideal viewer has lived through. Use sensory details and precise language that makes the viewer think, ‘That’s exactly me.’ Do not name the solution yet; sit in the pain for a moment.”* |
| **Specific Promise** | States a concrete, measurable outcome the viewer will gain | *“Open with a direct statement of exactly what the viewer will be able to do, understand, or avoid after watching this video. The promise must be specific and measurable—not ‘improve your productivity’ but ‘cut your weekly planning time from three hours to forty‑five minutes.’ Anchor the promise with a number or a concrete capability.”* |
| **Identity Challenge** | Naming a positive self‑view and showing how a habit undermines it | *“Open by naming a positive identity my target viewer holds about themselves (e.g., ‘you’re the kind of person who values deep focus’) and immediately suggest that a routine habit is silently working against that identity. Create a gap between who they think they are and what they’re actually doing.”* |
| **Open Loop** | Starts a story or scenario and breaks it off at the tensest moment | *“Open with the beginning of a specific personal story, a ‘what would you do if’ scenario, or a step‑by‑step process—and stop abruptly at the moment of highest tension or uncertainty. Do not provide any resolution. Signal that the resolution will arrive later in the video.”* |
