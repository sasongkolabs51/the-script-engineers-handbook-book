### Step 2: Understand Your Retention Mechanism

**Open Loop Extension** — The hook poses a question or mystery. The next 1–2 sentences *deepen* the mystery without solving it. The viewer's need for closure intensifies.

*Structure:* Hook (poses question) → Extension (adds a layer: "And it gets weirder" / "But that's not even the strangest part") → Forward signal ("I'll explain in a minute, but first...")

**Pattern Interrupt** — The hook sets up an expected narrative. The next sentence *breaks* that expectation. The viewer's predicted story is disrupted, forcing closer attention.

*Structure:* Hook (sets up expectation) → Interrupt ("But it wasn't [expected thing]. It was [unexpected thing].") → New question (the interrupt creates a NEW mystery)

**Stake Escalation** — The hook introduces a stake. The next sentences *raise* that stake dramatically, broadening relevance.

*Structure:* Hook (personal/specific stake) → Escalation ("But it's not just about [specific thing]. It's about [universal thing].") → Viewer connection ("And if you've ever [common experience], this applies to you.")

### Step 3: Build the Combined Prompt

```
Write the opening 15 seconds of a YouTube script (approximately
60–80 words, 4–6 sentences) for the following:

TOPIC: [your topic]
AUDIENCE: [your audience]
EMOTIONAL GOAL: [what the viewer should feel in these 15 seconds]

═══ HOOK (sentences 1–2) ═══
Archetype: [your chosen archetype]
Specific instruction: [paste the relevant instruction from Tool 2]

═══ RETENTION LOCK (sentences 3–5) ═══
Mechanism: [Open Loop Extension / Pattern Interrupt / Stake Escalation]
Specific instruction:
[Paste from below based on your choice:]

IF OPEN LOOP EXTENSION:
"The sentence after the hook must DEEPEN the mystery without
solving it. Add a layer: 'And here's the part nobody talks
about...' or 'But that's not even the strangest part.' Then
signal forward: 'I'll explain in [X] minutes, but first you
need to understand [setup for next section].' Do NOT reveal
the answer."

IF PATTERN INTERRUPT:
"The sentence after the hook must BREAK the expectation the
hook created. The viewer thinks the story is going one way.
Pivot: 'But it wasn't [expected]. It was [unexpected].' The
interrupt must create a NEW question that didn't exist before.
The viewer now has TWO reasons to keep watching."

IF STAKE ESCALATION:
"The sentence after the hook must RAISE the stakes from
personal/specific to universal. 'And this isn't just about
[my specific situation]. This is about [the viewer's broader
reality].' Connect the hook's specific story to the viewer's
life directly. Use 'you' language."

═══ CONSTRAINTS ═══
- Total length: 60–80 words. No more.
- The hook and retention lock must flow as ONE continuous
  thought. No jarring transition. No "Anyway, in this video..."
- Do NOT summarize what the video will cover.
- Do NOT greet the audience.
- Do NOT use: "Hey everyone," "Welcome back," "In today's
  video," "Let's dive in," "I'm excited to share."
- Tone: [your voice specification]
- End the 15-second block with a forward-pointing sentence
  that hands off to the body of the script.

Generate THREE complete versions (hook + retention lock together).
```
