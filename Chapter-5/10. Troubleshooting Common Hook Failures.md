## 10. Troubleshooting Common Hook Failures

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Hook feels generic, could be from any channel | Missing specificity; AI used broad statements | Add to prompt: “Include a specific number, date, name, or sensory detail that makes this hook uniquely personal.” |
| Retention chain breaks after hook (e.g., “So in this video…”) | Transition is a summary, not an escalation | Reinforce: “The sentence immediately after the hook must not summarize or explain the hook. It must deepen the mystery, raise stakes, or introduce a twist.” |
| Hook feels clickbaity or emotionally manipulative | Contrarian or Identity Challenge used too aggressively without grounding | Add: “The hook must feel like a genuine insight, not a provocation. Anchor the claim in the specific experience or evidence you’ll reveal in the video.” |
| AI ignores the retention mechanism | Mechanism described too abstractly | Provide explicit examples: e.g., “An Open Loop Extension means adding a sentence like ‘But what happened next wasn’t the lesson—it was something far weirder.’ Use a concrete example from my niche.” |
| All hooks sound the same across videos | Over‑reliance on a single archetype | Deliberately force a different archetype for the next video. Use the Hook Archetype Selection Worksheet to pick one outside your comfort zone. |
