## 6. Niche Engineering Lab Checklist

*Work through these five exercises to move from understanding to having a fully operational Niche Adaptation Blueprint.*

☐ **1. Build Your Primary Niche Blueprint**  
☐ Mapped my current niche’s structural, vocal, and rhythmic preferences using the Niche Archetype Quick‑Reference Card.  
☐ Filled out a Niche Adaptation Blueprint Builder for my primary niche, including voice modifications, pacing signature, and niche‑specific guardrails.  

☐ **2. Build a Secondary Niche Blueprint**  
☐ Chose a niche I want to expand into.  
☐ Studied three successful creators in that niche, noting their hooks, structure, emotional register, and pacing.  
☐ Filled out a Niche Adaptation Blueprint Builder for the secondary niche. This is my hypothesis.  

☐ **3. Run the Niche Switch Simulation**  
☐ Took a single topic kernel that could work in both niches.  
☐ Ran two Niche Switch prompts (one targeting each niche).  
☐ Compared outputs side by side using the Niche Switch Simulation Worksheet (see next page).  
☐ Refined the blueprint for any pillar that felt off.  

☐ **4. Create Your Cross‑Niche Safeguard List**  
☐ Reviewed my Voice Profile and identified the characteristics that are non‑negotiable across all niches.  
☐ Wrote a short list of “Always” and “Never” rules.  
☐ Appended this list to both blueprints.  

☐ **5. Self‑Assessment and Calibration**  
☐ Published a few videos using the secondary blueprint.  
☐ Reviewed retention data and audience comments.  
☐ Adjusted pacing signature, voice modifications, or framework choice based on real feedback.  
☐ Updated the blueprint and logged changes in the Calibration Log (see below).
