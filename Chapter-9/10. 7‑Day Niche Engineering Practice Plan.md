## 10. 7‑Day Niche Engineering Practice Plan

**Day 1 – Map Your Primary Niche**  
- [ ] Fill out the Niche Adaptation Blueprint Builder for your current main content type.  
- [ ] Document what you’ve already been doing implicitly. This becomes your baseline.

**Day 2 – Study a New Niche**  
- [ ] Pick a secondary niche you’d like to enter (or one you occasionally touch).  
- [ ] Watch three successful creators in that niche. Note their hooks, structures, emotional tones, and pacing rhythms.  
- [ ] Begin filling out a blueprint for this niche based on your observations.

**Day 3 – Complete the Secondary Blueprint**  
- [ ] Finish the Niche Adaptation Blueprint, Voice Modification Worksheet, and Pacing Signature for the new niche.  
- [ ] Write a full Niche Switch prompt for it and save it in your Prompt Lab.

**Day 4 – Simulation Day**  
- [ ] Choose a universal kernel topic.  
- [ ] Run two Niche Switch prompts: one for your primary niche, one for the new niche.  
- [ ] Use the Simulation Worksheet to compare outputs. Note any mismatches.

**Day 5 – Refine and Tighten**  
- [ ] Based on yesterday’s observations, adjust the secondary blueprint—voice settings, pacing tokens, guardrails.  
- [ ] Rerun the Niche Switch prompt and re‑evaluate. Aim for both feeling authentic *and* niche‑appropriate.

**Day 6 – Build the Safeguard List**  
- [ ] Write your Cross‑Niche Safeguard List. Identify the 5–7 identity markers you will never change.  
- [ ] Attach it to every blueprint. Also paste it into your main Voice Profile document.

**Day 7 – Live Test (Optional but Recommended)**  
- [ ] Create a real video for the new niche using your adapted system.  
- [ ] Monitor retention and comments. Ask: “Does the audience feel I belong in this niche?”  
- [ ] If yes, lock the blueprint. If no, return to Day 5 and iterate.
