## TOOL 9: Troubleshooting Guide — Niche Adaptation Failures

### *When the adaptation feels wrong. Specific symptoms, specific fixes.*

---

| Symptom | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Script sounds like a different person — I don't recognize myself | Over-adapted. Voice modifications went too far. Safeguards were violated. | Re-read your Cross-Niche Safeguards (Tool 6). Check: did the niche modification override a safeguard? Revert. The niche adapts to YOU, not the reverse. |
| Script sounds like me but feels wrong for the audience | Under-adapted. Niche parameters not applied strongly enough. | Increase the niche-specific modifications. If the pacing is too fast for a vlog, slow it MORE. If the emotional temperature is too cool for a story, warm it MORE. Push the dials further. |
| Hook doesn't signal the niche — viewer might be confused about what type of video this is | Hook archetype mismatch. Using a review-style hook for a vlog, or an educational hook for a reaction video. | Switch to the niche-appropriate hook archetype (Tool 1). A vlog needs an Open Loop or Relatable Pain hook. A review needs a Contrarian Claim or Curiosity Gap. |
| Pacing feels "off" but I can't name why | Tempo mismatch with audience expectations. Too fast for a reflective audience, too slow for an entertainment audience. | Check the niche pacing preset (Tool 4). Adjust overall tempo by 2 points on the 1–10 scale. Re-run Layer 3. Read aloud. Does it feel native now? |
| B-roll feels wrong — too informational for a story, too atmospheric for a tutorial | B-roll style mismatch. | Switch B-roll style per niche: informational for educational/review, atmospheric for story/vlog, source clips for reaction. Update the B-roll instruction in Layer 3. |
| Voice sounds right in the hook but drifts into the "wrong" register by the middle | Niche voice modification not sustained across the full script. | Add to Layer 4 prompt: "Maintain the [niche] emotional register consistently across ALL sections. Do not revert to baseline in body sections. The [niche] register is the default for this entire script." |
| The script feels like a "costume" — I'm performing a niche rather than inhabiting it | The adaptation is too mechanical. You're following rules without internalizing the audience's emotional needs. | Before prompting, spend 2 minutes imagining you're talking to ONE person in this niche's audience. What do they need to feel? What are they afraid of? What do they hope for? Prompt from that empathy, not just the parameter sheet. |
| I keep defaulting to my primary niche's settings even when targeting a secondary niche | Habit. The primary blueprint is so ingrained that you forget to switch. | Before every script, physically open the correct Adaptation Blueprint (Tool 2). Read it. Load the parameters. Make the switch EXPLICIT, not assumed. |
| Audience comments say "this doesn't feel like you" after a niche switch | You over-adapted and lost your identity. OR you under-adapted and the niche feels wrong. | Check retention data: if they drop at the hook, the niche signal is wrong. If they drop in the middle, the voice/pacing is off. If they comment "not like you," a safeguard was violated. |

---

### The "Am I Still Me?" Quick Check

After generating a niche-adapted script, ask:

> "If my most loyal subscriber watched this without knowing I made it, would they recognize it as mine within 30 seconds?"

☐ Yes → The adaptation is working. Ship it.
☐ Maybe → Check your safeguards. Is your colloquial fingerprint intact? Your sentence rhythm? Your humor type?
☐ No → You've over-adapted. Pull back. Reduce the niche modifications by 30%. Re-run.
