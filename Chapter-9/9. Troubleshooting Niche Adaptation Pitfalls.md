## 9. Troubleshooting Niche Adaptation Pitfalls

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| The voice feels like a different person, not “me” in a different mode. | Over‑adaptation; you changed core Colloquial Fingerprint. | Re‑examine your Cross‑Niche Safeguard List. Ensure signature phrases, sentence architecture, and cultural references remain untouched. Only adjust energy and emphasis, not identity. |
| The script structure feels generic, not tailored to the niche. | Blueprint framework too broad or not enforced in the prompt. | Specify the exact beat script in the Niche Switch prompt. Use the beat names and mandatory fields from Chapter 6. |
| Pacing feels off—too fast for a vlog, too slow for a reaction video. | Pacing Signature not specific enough, or default tokens overridden. | Tighten the Pacing Signature: add a beats‑per‑minute feel description, list forbidden tokens explicitly, and give the AI a reference video’s pacing as an example. |
| The hook doesn’t grab the niche audience. | You used a hook archetype that doesn’t match the niche’s psychological contract. | Re‑check the Reference Card. For story‑driven content, Open Loop and Relatable Pain are strong; avoid Contrarian Claim unless it’s a personal, lived contrarianism. |
| Adaptation feels like a different creator’s style, not mine. | You may have unconsciously copied another creator’s blueprint rather than adapting your own. | Return to your Voice Profile and modify *your* settings, not someone else’s. Your emotional range should stretch, not be replaced. |
