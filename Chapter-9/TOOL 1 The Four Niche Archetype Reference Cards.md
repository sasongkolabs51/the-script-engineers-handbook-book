## TOOL 1: The Four Niche Archetype Reference Cards

### *The default configuration for each major content type. Your starting point for every adaptation.*

---

### ARCHETYPE A: Educational / How-To

**The audience wants:** To understand something. To gain a skill. To feel progressively more capable.

| Pillar | Default Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Framework** | PAS (with expanded Solution beats) or sequential Process breakdown. Clarity and logical progression are paramount. |
| **Hook Archetypes** | Curiosity Gap ("Here's something counterintuitive about this skill") or Specific Promise ("By the end, you'll be able to do X in Y minutes") |
| **Emotional Register** | Curious → Empowering. The viewer should feel smarter at the end than the start. Warm, encouraging, patient. |
| **Voice Modifications** | Dial warmth UP slightly. Reduce humor density (keep type). Increase clarity of explanations. Add niche-specific vocabulary. Maintain all colloquial fingerprints. |
| **Pacing Signature** | Moderate tempo. [Slow emphasis] on key concepts. [Text on screen] for steps/frameworks. [B-roll] demonstrating the process. Minimal [Hard cut]. Clear signposting between sections. |
| **B-Roll Style** | Screen recordings, demonstrations, step-by-step visuals, annotated diagrams. Informational, not atmospheric. |
| **Niche Guardrails** | "Never introduce a concept without a concrete example." "Every step must be immediately actionable." "No jargon without a plain-language translation." |

---

### ARCHETYPE B: Reaction / Entertainment

**The audience wants:** To share an emotional experience. Surprise, laughter, indignation, delight. To feel like they're watching it WITH you.

| Pillar | Default Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Framework** | Hook-Story-Offer (simplified) or Reaction-Insight-Application. Emotional volatility is the structure. |
| **Hook Archetypes** | Contrarian Claim ("Everyone loves this—I think it's secretly terrible") or Relatable Pain ("This is the moment every creator dreads") |
| **Emotional Register** | High-variability. Swing between genuine shock, sharp critique, exuberant appreciation, and amused detachment. Never flat. Never one note. |
| **Voice Modifications** | Increase emotional temperature VARIATION (not baseline). Increase humor density. Maintain humor TYPE. More fragments, snappier rhythms. Allow exclamations (sparingly). |
| **Pacing Signature** | Rapid-fire. [Fast energy] dominant. [Reaction beat] after every surprising moment. [Pause 0.5s] for comedic timing. [Hard cut] between reaction segments. Few [Slow down] moments (save for genuine insight). |
| **B-Roll Style** | Source material clips, reaction cam, split-screens, quick cuts, meme inserts. High visual energy. |
| **Niche Guardrails** | "Every reaction must be anchored to a specific moment in the source material." "Never react vaguely—name the exact timestamp or element." "Genuine emotion over performed emotion." |

---

### ARCHETYPE C: Story-Driven / Vlog

**The audience wants:** Emotional connection. Narrative meaning. To feel they've shared a genuine human experience. Catharsis.

| Pillar | Default Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Framework** | Hero's Journey (7-beat) or personal transformation arc. Significant time on the Ordeal and the Return. |
| **Hook Archetypes** | Open Loop ("I'm going to tell you about the worst day of my professional life") or Relatable Pain (dropping into a specific vulnerable moment) |
| **Emotional Register** | Vulnerable, empathetic, reflective. The viewer should feel they're hearing a confession, not watching a presentation. Warmth over authority. |
| **Voice Modifications** | Shift emotional temperature toward vulnerable end of YOUR range. Reduce humor density (when humor appears, it's gentler). Allow longer sentences. More [Whisper] moments. Maintain colloquial fingerprint as the identity anchor. |
| **Pacing Signature** | Slow, reflective. [Pause 1.5s] and [Pause 3s] at emotional peaks. [Slow down] before revelations. [Whisper] for vulnerable admissions. [Reaction beat] after confessions. Fewer [Hard cut]. [Fade out energy] for the landing. |
| **B-Roll Style** | Atmospheric, not informational. Environmental shots, hands doing something, empty rooms, walking footage, time-lapses. Mood over data. |
| **Niche Guardrails** | "Never rush an emotional beat." "Vulnerability is stated plainly, not performed." "The story must have a specific scene, not a vague summary." "Silence is a tool—use it." |

---

### ARCHETYPE D: Review / Comparison

**The audience wants:** A trustworthy evaluation. Authority. Side-by-side analysis. A clear verdict to inform a decision.

| Pillar | Default Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Framework** | Modified PAS or Compare-Contrast structure. Criteria → Evaluation → Verdict. Analytical and balanced. |
| **Hook Archetypes** | Contrarian Claim ("The cheaper option is actually better for most people") or Curiosity Gap ("I found a flaw that no one is talking about") |
| **Emotional Register** | Authoritative, discerning, honest. The viewer should feel they're getting an expert assessment from someone who's actually tested the thing. |
| **Voice Modifications** | Shift toward cooler, more analytical end of YOUR range. Authority through precision, not formality. Humor becomes sharp observations. Maintain colloquial fingerprint. Add product-specific vocabulary. |
| **Pacing Signature** | Crisp, efficient. [Punch] on key specs and numbers. [Hard cut] between comparison points. [Text on screen] for specs/scores. [Slow emphasis] on the final verdict. Minimal [Pause 3s]. [Fast energy] through spec lists. |
| **B-Roll Style** | Product close-ups, spec comparisons, benchmark results, side-by-side shots, interface demonstrations. Precise, informational. |
| **Niche Guardrails** | "Every claim must be backed by a specific test, number, or observation." "Never say 'it's good' without saying WHY and FOR WHOM." "Present both sides before delivering the verdict." "Translate specs into user-experience consequences." |
