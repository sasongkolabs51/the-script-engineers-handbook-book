## 4. AI Prompt Templates

### 4.1 Voice Pattern Extraction Prompt

Use this on each of your three writing samples to get a structured analysis without filling out the worksheet manually.

```
Analyze the following text for voice patterns. Be extremely specific and avoid vague adjectives. Provide a structured specification covering these four dimensions:

1. SENTENCE ARCHITECTURE: Average sentence length, pattern of variation (long‑short‑long, etc.), use of fragments, punctuation quirks, common sentence openers, and whether passive voice is used.

2. HUMOR DENSITY AND TYPE: Identify every joke, wry observation, or playful aside. Note the mechanism (self‑deprecation, absurd exaggeration, deadpan understatement, cultural reference, wordplay). Estimate the percentage of paragraphs that contain humor. Describe how humor is delivered (signposted or deadpan).

3. EMOTIONAL TEMPERATURE: Baseline register (e.g., warm, analytical, skeptical). How does the tone shift across the text? What emotions are present or avoided?

4. COLLOQUIAL FINGERPRINT: List signature words and phrases, words that are never used, contraction usage, cultural reference universe, and any grammatical habits (e.g., addresses the viewer as "you," uses "honestly" as a discourse marker).

Text:
[PASTE YOUR WRITING SAMPLE]
```

### 4.2 Voice Profile Synthesis Prompt

After you’ve run the extraction on three samples, use this to get a first draft of a unified profile.

```
Below are three voice specifications extracted from different writing samples of the same creator. Synthesize them into a single, unified Voice Profile. Identify the patterns that appear consistently across all three and discard one‑off anomalies unless they feel essential to the creator’s identity.

The unified profile must cover these four dimensions in clear, prompt‑ready language:

1. Sentence Architecture
2. Humor Density and Type
3. Emotional Temperature
4. Colloquial Fingerprint

The output should be a specification I can paste directly into a prompt to instruct an AI to write in this voice.

Specification 1:
[PASTE]

Specification 2:
[PASTE]

Specification 3:
[PASTE]
```

### 4.3 Permanent Layer 4 Voice Overlay Prompt

This becomes your default Layer 4 prompt. Paste your Voice Profile and the paced script.

```
Rewrite the following script using the Voice Profile below. Maintain ALL pacing tokens ([Pause 1s], [Fast energy], etc.), structural beats, and hook phrasing exactly as written. Do not alter the emotional arc or the sequence of information. Only change the surface language—word choice, sentence rhythm, and phrasing—to match the voice described in the profile.

Voice Profile:
[PASTE YOUR PERMANENT VOICE PROFILE]

Script:
[PASTE YOUR LAYER 3 PACED SCRIPT]
```

### 4.4 Voice Debugging Prompt

When a section of the voiced output doesn’t sound like you, use this to diagnose and fix it.

```
The following paragraph from an AI‑voiced script doesn’t sound like me. My Voice Profile is pasted below. Please:

1. Identify which dimension(s) of my Voice Profile this paragraph violates or fails to capture.
2. Explain why the language feels off based on the profile.
3. Rewrite the paragraph to fully comply with the profile while preserving the original meaning and any pacing tokens.

My Voice Profile:
[PASTE YOUR VOICE PROFILE]

Offending paragraph:
[PASTE THE PARAGRAPH]
```
