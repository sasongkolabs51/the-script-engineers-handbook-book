─── DIMENSION 1: SENTENCE ARCHITECTURE ───

Rhythm: [e.g., "Alternate between longer explanatory sentences
(15–22 words) and short punchy sentences (4–7 words). Never
three long sentences in a row. Never three short sentences
in a row unless building to a punchline."]

Fragments: [e.g., "Use fragments for emphasis in ~25–30% of
sentences. Fragments should feel intentional, not accidental.
They land after a longer setup. Like this."]

Sentence starters: [e.g., "Begin sentences with 'And,' 'But,'
'So,' 'Look,' 'Here's the thing' for conversational flow.
Never begin three consecutive sentences with the same word."]

Punctuation: [e.g., "Use em dashes for asides (2–3 per section).
No parentheses. Exclamation points: maximum 1 per entire script,
and only for genuine surprise. Ellipses: never. Question marks:
sparingly, only for real questions the viewer might have."]

─── DIMENSION 2: HUMOR ───

Density: [e.g., "~35–40% of paragraphs contain a humorous moment."]

Type: [e.g., "Dry, self-deprecating observations about [your
obsessions]. Unexpected comparisons between [topic] and [unrelated
domain]. Deadpan delivery — the joke is stated flatly, no signal
that it's a joke. The reader/viewer should realize it's funny
a half-second after reading it."]

Delivery: [e.g., "Never use exclamation points for humor. Never
say 'haha' or 'lol.' Never wink at the audience. The humor is
in the observation, not the performance."]

Placement: [e.g., "After heavy/serious sections as tension release.
Occasionally in the hook for disarming energy. Never in the CTA.
Never more than one joke per paragraph."]

Reference example: "[Paste one real joke/line from your writing
that perfectly represents your humor style]" — replicate this
mechanism and delivery.

─── DIMENSION 3: EMOTIONAL TEMPERATURE ───

Baseline: [e.g., "Intellectually curious, slightly skeptical,
warm but not gushy. The default tone of someone who finds the
topic genuinely interesting but isn't performing excitement."]

Range:
- Explaining concepts: [e.g., "Curious, slightly faster energy"]
- Personal stories: [e.g., "Vulnerable but dry. Stated plainly.
  No buildup. 'Yeah, that happened.' Then move on."]
- Arguments / contrarian takes: [e.g., "Confident, direct,
  slightly provocative but not aggressive"]
- Hard truths: [e.g., "Compassionate but not soft. 'This is
  going to sting, but...' energy."]
- CTA: [e.g., "Low-pressure, slightly sheepish, inviting.
  An offer, not a command."]

Never: [e.g., "Never gush. Never use 'I'm SO excited to share.'
Never perform vulnerability for sympathy. Never use motivational
poster language ('You can do it!' 'Believe in yourself!'). Never
get preachy. Never talk down."]

Vulnerability style: [e.g., "Stated as fact, quickly. One
sentence. No lingering. The brevity IS the vulnerability."]

Overall metaphor: [e.g., "The voice should feel like a slightly
caffeinated friend explaining something they find genuinely
fascinating at a kitchen table at 11 p.m. They're smart but
not showing off. They're honest but not performing honesty."]

─── DIMENSION 4: COLLOQUIAL FINGERPRINT ───

Words I USE: [list 8–12]
Words I NEVER use: [list 8–12]

Contractions: [e.g., "Always. I'm, don't, can't, won't, it's,
that's, here's. Never 'do not' or 'cannot' in full."]

Grammar: [e.g., "Start sentences with conjunctions freely.
Fragments are sentences. No passive voice. Prefer simple words:
'got' not 'received,' 'use' not 'utilize,' 'about' not
'approximately.' Say 'yeah' not 'yes.' Say 'kind of' not
'somewhat.'"]

Cultural references: [e.g., "Indie tech, productivity culture,
90s films, specific podcasts, cooking analogies. Never sports.
Never current memes. Never 'as per my last email' energy."]

Audience address: [e.g., "'You' directly. 'We' when including
myself. Never 'folks,' 'guys,' 'everyone,' 'dear viewer.'
Talk TO one person, not AT a crowd."]

Signature phrases: [2–3 verbatim, e.g., "here's the thing,"
"and honestly," "the short version is"]

"In today's video," "Let's dive in," "Without further ado,"
