## TOOL 1: Voice Sample Collection Guide

### *Gather the raw material. Three samples. Your actual writing. Not what you think you sound like—what you actually sound like.*

---

### What You Need

Three samples of your own writing that authentically represent your voice. Not aspirational writing. Not "how I wish I sounded." The real thing.

**Best sources (pick three):**

☐ Scripts from videos you've recorded and published (ideal—this is your performed voice)
☐ Newsletter emails or blog posts you wrote without overthinking
☐ Long-form social media posts (threads, captions) that got good engagement
☐ Voice memos you've transcribed (your literal speaking voice)
☐ DMs or emails to friends where you were explaining something you care about
☐ Comments you've left on other creators' videos (your reactive voice)

**Avoid:**

☐ Writing you heavily edited or got help with
☐ Writing from more than 2 years ago (your voice has evolved)
☐ Writing you did for a client or in a professional/formal context
☐ Writing you're embarrassed by (you'll self-censor the extraction)

### The Gut Check

Before running the extraction, read all three samples aloud. One after another.

**Do they sound like the same person?** ☐ Yes ☐ Mostly ☐ Not really

**If "not really":** Which sample sounds most like the "real" you? Which sounds like a performance? Note this. The Voice Profile should capture the *real* you, not the performance.
