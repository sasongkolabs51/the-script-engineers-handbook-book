## 5. Voice Drift Diagnostic Card

When you sense something is off in a voiced script but can’t immediately name it, run through this checklist. Each symptom points to a specific dimension that needs tightening.

| Symptom | Likely Dimension | Check / Fix |
|---------|------------------|-------------|
| Sentences feel too uniform; no rhythm variety. | Sentence Architecture | Check if your profile specifies variation pattern and fragment frequency. Add: “Vary sentence length dramatically. Use fragments for punch.” |
| Humor feels forced, or jokes are absent where you’d naturally have them. | Humor Density and Type | Check density percentage and mechanism. Add an example joke you’ve actually used. Specify delivery style. |
| Tone is emotionally flat—no warmth where there should be, or no sharpness. | Emotional Temperature | Check baseline and range. Add explicit trigger words and phrases for emotional shifts. Clarify what emotions to avoid. |
| Generic words like “very,” “really,” “folks,” “leverage” appear. | Colloquial Fingerprint | Expand banned word list. Add signature replacement phrases (“wild” instead of “very surprising”). |
| The voice sounds like a generic presenter, not you specifically. | Multiple dimensions | Run the full Calibration Loop again with fresh samples. Your profile may be under‑specified overall. |
| Voice is consistent but feels like a flattened, “safe” version of you. | Emotional Temperature | Widen the range. Let the profile include your full expressive spectrum—irreverence, passion, quiet vulnerability—not just the middle. |
