## 2. Reusable Prompt Blocks (Copy‑Paste Ready)

*Keep these blocks in a pinned note for quick access. Fill the bracketed parts before sending.*

**Layer 1 — Ideation**  
*“I am creating a [type of video] for [audience]. The topic is: [topic]. The angle: [angle]. The emotional outcome I want: [feeling]. Structure: [framework]. Generate a structured outline with five to seven key beats. No tone or stylistic requests.”*

**Layer 2 — Structural Skeleton**  
*“Using the following beat sheet, write a complete script skeleton in raw, functional prose. Do not add jokes, stylized language, or rhythm flourishes. Focus exclusively on clear logic, smooth transitions, and information flow. Include [HOOK] and [CTA] placeholders at the appropriate positions.”*

**Layer 3 — Pacing Injection**  
*“Inject pacing and delivery markers into this script skeleton. Use the following tokens where appropriate: [Pause 1s], [Pause 2s], [Fast energy], [Slow down], [B‑roll moment: describe what should appear], [Reaction beat], [Whisper emphasis], [Laugh break]. Vary sentence lengths to create urgency in high‑stakes moments and breathing room in reflective sections. The final output should feel like a performance script a presenter could read directly, with natural rhythm and visual cues embedded.”*

**Layer 4 — Voice Overlay**  
*“Rewrite the following script using my specific voice. Characteristics: [your voice attributes]. Maintain all pacing tokens and structural beats exactly as written. The goal is to make this sound like me talking to a friend, not like a presenter reading a script.”*
