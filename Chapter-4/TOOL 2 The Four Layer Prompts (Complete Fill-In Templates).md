## TOOL 2: The Four Layer Prompts (Complete Fill-In Templates)

### LAYER 1 PROMPT: Ideation

```
I am creating a [platform: YouTube / TikTok / podcast / etc.] video
for [target audience: be specific — age, knowledge level, what
they're struggling with, what they've already tried].

TOPIC: [Your specific topic — not "productivity" but "why the
3-week productivity app cycle keeps repeating"]

ANGLE: [Your specific take / contrarian framing / unique lens.
What are you arguing that others aren't? Why does YOUR perspective
matter here?]

EMOTIONAL OUTCOME: [What should the viewer FEEL by the end?
Not "informed." Be specific: "empowered and slightly angry,"
"relieved and seen," "curious enough to try one specific thing
today."]

NARRATIVE STRUCTURE: [Choose one or combine:
- Contrarian Claim → Evidence Stack → Reframe → Actionable Takeaway
- Relatable Pain → Hidden Mechanism → Personal Confession → Reframe → Shift
- Hook-Story-Offer
- Problem → Agitation → Solution
- Reaction → Insight → Application
- Or describe your own: _______________________________]

PERSONAL ELEMENT: [What's YOUR story, experience, or vulnerability
that anchors this? One sentence describing the personal beat you'll
include.]

Generate a structured beat sheet with 5–7 key beats. For each beat,
provide:
- Beat number and label
- The narrative function it serves (Hook / Stakes / Tension Loop /
  Insight / Escalation / Payoff / CTA)
- 2–3 sentences describing what this beat covers
- The emotional temperature (e.g., "curious," "uncomfortable,"
  "relieved," "energized")

Do NOT write prose. Do NOT add tone or style. Do NOT write hooks
or CTAs. Only the structural blueprint.
```

### LAYER 2 PROMPT: Structural Skeleton

```
Using the following beat sheet, write a complete script skeleton
in raw, functional prose.

[PASTE YOUR APPROVED BEAT SHEET FROM LAYER 1 HERE]

INSTRUCTIONS:
- Each beat becomes 1–3 paragraphs of clear, logical prose.
- Focus EXCLUSIVELY on: argument strength, information flow,
  logical transitions, and completeness of ideas.
- Do NOT add jokes, stylized language, rhythm flourishes, or
  personality. This skeleton should read like a clear, deliberately
  bland first draft.
- Include [HOOK] placeholder at the opening position.
- Include [CTA] placeholder at the closing position.
- Include [TENSION LOOP] placeholder where an unanswered question
  should be planted.
- Mark any point where a specific example, data point, or anecdote
  should go with [SPECIFIC DETAIL NEEDED: describe what].
- Total length target: approximately [___] words.

The skeleton should work as a boring but logically airtight document.
If the argument holds in monotone, it will sing once layered with
pacing and voice. If it's confused here, decoration will only make
it worse.
```

### LAYER 3 PROMPT: Pacing Injection

```
Take the following script skeleton and inject pacing and delivery
markers to transform it into a performable script.

[PASTE YOUR APPROVED SKELETON FROM LAYER 2 HERE]

PACING TOKEN LIBRARY — use these where appropriate:
- [PAUSE 1s] — brief beat before a key point
- [PAUSE 2s] — longer silence for emphasis or emotional weight
- [FAST ENERGY] — accelerate delivery, shorter sentences, urgency
- [SLOW DOWN] — decelerate, longer sentences, reflective weight
- [B-ROLL: describe what appears on screen] — visual break /
  illustration moment
- [REACTION BEAT] — presenter reacts physically (face, gesture)
- [WHISPER EMPHASIS] — drop volume for intimacy or secrecy
- [LAUGH BREAK] — moment of levity, natural chuckle
- [BEAT] — micro-pause, less than a second, for comedic or
  dramatic timing

ADDITIONAL PACING INSTRUCTIONS:
- Vary sentence length deliberately: follow any sentence over
  18 words with one under 8 words in high-energy sections.
- In reflective / vulnerable sections, allow longer sentences
  (20–25 words) with natural breathing rhythm.
- Place [PAUSE] tokens BEFORE revelations, not after.
- Place [B-ROLL] at logical visual breaks (after a claim that
  needs illustration, during a list, at a transition).
- The script should feel like a performance a presenter could
  read directly, with natural rhythm and visual cues embedded.
- Do NOT change the content, argument, or structure. Only add
  rhythm and delivery architecture.

EMOTIONAL STAGE DIRECTIONS (adjust per section):
- [Hook section]: [e.g., "sharp, slightly confrontational energy"]
- [Stakes section]: [e.g., "personal, vulnerable, slower"]
- [Body sections]: [e.g., "building energy, confident"]
- [Payoff section]: [e.g., "warm, resolved, slightly triumphant"]
- [CTA]: [e.g., "casual, inviting, low-pressure"]
```

### LAYER 4 PROMPT: Voice Overlay

```
Rewrite the following paced script using my specific voice profile.
Preserve ALL pacing tokens, structural beats, and [B-ROLL] markers
exactly as written. You are revoicing the prose around the tokens,
not removing or relocating them.

[PASTE YOUR APPROVED PACED SCRIPT FROM LAYER 3 HERE]

MY VOICE PROFILE:
- Perspective: [first-person / second-person address / mix]
- Register: [e.g., "conversational but intellectually sharp —
  like explaining something fascinating to a smart friend over
  bad coffee"]
- Sentence habits: [e.g., "I start sentences with 'And' or 'But'
  for flow. I use fragments for emphasis. Like this. I never do
  three long sentences in a row."]
- Humor style: [e.g., "dry, observational, self-deprecating.
  Never puns. Never 'haha' energy. More 'yeah, that was stupid'
  than 'OMG so funny!']
- Vocabulary I USE: [list 5–10 words/phrases that are yours]
- Vocabulary I NEVER use: [list 5–10 words/phrases to avoid]
- Cultural references: [e.g., "indie tech, productivity culture,
  90s movies, obscure podcast references"]
- Emotional register: [e.g., "honest but not performative. I'll
  admit failure dryly. 'Yeah, I messed that up' not 'I want to
  take you on a journey through my pain.'"]
- Banned phrases: [e.g., "In today's video," "Let's dive in,"
  "Without further ado," "comprehensive," "delve," "landscape,"
  "game-changer"]

CONSTRAINTS:
- Keep all [PAUSE], [FAST ENERGY], [SLOW DOWN], [B-ROLL],
  [REACTION BEAT], [WHISPER EMPHASIS], [LAUGH BREAK], and
  [BEAT] tokens in their exact positions.
- Keep all structural beats and argument logic unchanged.
- The output should sound like me TALKING, not like a script
  being READ.
- If any section feels uncertain, flag it with [VOICE CHECK]
  so I can make the final call.
```
