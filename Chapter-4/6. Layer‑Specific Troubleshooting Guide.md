## 6. Layer‑Specific Troubleshooting Guide

| **Symptom** | **Likely Cause** | **Fix** |
|-------------|------------------|---------|
| Beat sheet feels generic or disjointed | The angle wasn’t sharp enough, or you let the AI pick the structure | Add a contrarian, personal angle; specify the framework explicitly in Layer 1 |
| Skeleton reads like a Wikipedia summary | The AI defaulted to informational mode; no guardrails | Add to Layer 2: “At each beat, include a specific, unexpected concrete detail or personal anecdote” |
| Pacing tokens feel random or robotic | You didn’t tell the AI the emotional intention behind each section | In Layer 3, add stage directions: “Beat 3 (confession) should feel slow, vulnerable. Beat 5 (reframe) should feel urgent but clear.” |
| Voice overlay sounds generic or overly formal | Your voice description was too abstract (e.g., “conversational”) | Replace with concrete traits: “first‑person, uses sentence fragments, starts sentences with ‘And’ or ‘But,’ uses cultural references to indie tech” |
| Voice overlay broke the pacing tokens | The AI reinterpreted or removed them | In Layer 4, explicitly say: “Do not alter, remove, or move any pacing tokens or structural placeholders. Change only the surrounding language.” |
