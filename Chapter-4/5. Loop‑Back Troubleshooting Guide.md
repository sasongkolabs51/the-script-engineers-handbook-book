## 5. Loop‑Back Troubleshooting Guide

When a layer output isn’t right, don’t start over. Isolate and fix.

| Symptom | Layer | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|--------------|-----|
| Beat sheet feels generic, lacks point of view | 1 | Angle too broad or safe | Add: “The angle must be a contrarian or deeply personal take. Do not use a perspective a generic article could take.” |
| Skeleton reads like a Wikipedia summary, no personal stakes | 2 | No instruction to include concrete details | Add a guardrail: “At each beat, you must include a specific, unexpected concrete detail or personal reference.” |
| Skeleton transitions are jarring or illogical | 2 | Beat order wrong or transitions ignored | Add: “Ensure each paragraph ends with a sentence that naturally leads to the next. If needed, add a bridging sentence.” |
| Pacing tokens feel randomly placed, unnatural to read aloud | 3 | Emotional intention of sections not specified | Return to Layer 3 and add stage directions: “The confession beat should feel slow and vulnerable; the reframe beat should build to an energetic, confident climax.” |
| Pacing is uniform—no variation in energy | 3 | Prompt didn’t emphasize rhythm variety | Add: “Vary sentence length dramatically. Use one‑word sentences for impact, then longer, flowing sentences. Mark high‑energy and low‑energy sections explicitly.” |
| Voice overlay drifts into formal or generic language | 4 | Voice description too abstract (“conversational,” “friendly”) | Replace with concrete characteristics: “Use contractions, sentence fragments, and occasional mild swearing for emphasis. Start 30% of sentences with ‘And’ or ‘But.’” |
| Voice overlay preserved pacing but broke structure | 4 | AI misinterpreted “rewrite” as permission to restructure | Add explicit boundary: “Do not change the order of beats, sections, or any structural element. Only change word choice and phrasing to match my voice.” |
