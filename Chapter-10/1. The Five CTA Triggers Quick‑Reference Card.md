## 1. The Five CTA Triggers Quick‑Reference Card

*Use these trigger types to make any request feel like a natural extension of the viewer’s emotional journey. Each trigger includes a prompt instruction you can paste directly into your Payoff Generator.*

| **Trigger** | **What It Does** | **Prompt Instruction (paste into your payoff prompt)** |
|-------------|------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|
| **Identity Reinforcement** | Connects subscribing to the viewer’s positive self‑image. | “Frame the CTA as an invitation to join a community defined by a shared value or identity that the video has just reinforced. The viewer should feel that subscribing is an expression of a positive trait they already possess.” |
| **Curiosity Bridge** | Creates an information gap that only the next video can close. | “End the video by previewing a specific, concrete detail or question that the next video will address. Make the viewer curious enough that subscribing feels like the only way to ensure they don’t miss the answer.” |
| **Social Proof Nudge** | Leverages others’ behavior without sounding needy. | “Mention a specific, positive community behavior or a credible metric (like ‘thousands of creators’ or ‘the discussions in the comments’) that signals the channel is worth joining, without begging for validation.” |
| **Empowered Utility** | Positions subscribing as a tool for the viewer’s own growth. | “Present subscribing as an act of self‑interest—a way for the viewer to gain more practical value with zero additional effort. The emphasis must be on what they gain, not what you gain.” |
| **Emotional Debt Repayment** | Asks the viewer to pay forward the emotional gift they just received. | “After a particularly vulnerable or impactful section, make a single, soft ask that frames sharing as an act of generosity to someone else, not as support for the channel. The tone must be humble and sincere, never transactional.” |
