## TOOL 8: The Payoff Troubleshooting Guide

### *When the ending still feels off. Specific symptoms, specific fixes.*

---

| Symptom | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| CTA sounds robotic / like a different person | No trigger specified. Prompt said "write a CTA" instead of using a trigger-specific instruction. | Use Tool 1 templates. Specify the trigger by name. "Frame the CTA as an Identity Reinforcement trigger" produces dramatically different output than "ask them to subscribe." |
| Ending feels disconnected from the script | Emotional Resolution Arc missing. Jumped straight from final beat to CTA. | Add the 3 micro-beats (Tool 2). The viewer needs 20–30 seconds of "exhale" before any ask. The Resolution Arc IS the bridge. |
| Session flow callback feels forced / gimmicky | Callback is too literal—repeating the hook verbatim. | Echo the THEME or LANGUAGE, not the exact words. If the hook said "43 apps," the callback might say "two survivors" without repeating "43." The echo should feel like a knowing nod, not a copy-paste. |
| Voice drifts into formality at the end | Voice Profile losing salience after long scripts. AI defaults to "presenter voice" for CTAs. | Add to the CTA prompt: "Before outputting, verify every sentence matches my Voice Profile. Replace any formal constructions with my colloquial fingerprint. The CTA sounds like ME talking to a friend, not a presenter addressing an audience." |
| CTA feels too long / viewer drops off | Over-written. Too many asks. Too much explanation. | Cut to 40–60 words max. ONE ask. ONE trigger. The Resolution Arc does the emotional work. The CTA is just the final, gentle nudge. |
| Ending feels manipulative / guilt-trippy | Using obligation language or making it about the creator's needs. | Remove all "it helps the channel," "I work really hard on these," "please consider." Replace with viewer-benefit language: "You'll get [X]" or "Send this to someone who needs it." |
| The callback doesn't land — viewer doesn't register the loop | Callback placed too early or too subtly. | Place the callback in the Emotional Landing (Micro-Beat 1) or the Forward Gaze (Micro-Beat 3). Make it unmistakable: use the same key word or image from the hook. The viewer should think "oh, THAT'S what that was about." |
| Pacing feels wrong at the end — too rushed or too slow | Niche mismatch. Using review pacing for a vlog ending, or vlog pacing for a review. | Check Tool 5 (Niche Payoff Configurations). Match the ending pacing to the niche. Story/vlog = slow, reflective. Review = crisp, confident. Reaction = energetic, quick. |
| All three CTA variants sound the same | Prompt too constrained. Not enough variation instruction. | Add: "The three variants must differ in EMOTIONAL TONE: Variant A is confident and direct. Variant B is warm and invitational. Variant C is quiet and vulnerable. Same trigger, different energy." |
| Viewer retention drops in the final 10 seconds | The CTA is too long, too salesy, or the video effectively "ended" before the CTA started. | Shorten the CTA. Make the Resolution Arc do more work. The CTA should be 1–2 sentences max after the Resolution Arc. If the viewer feels the video is "over," they leave. Keep them in the spell until the final word. |

---

### The Emergency Ending Fix (15 minutes from recording)

If your ending is broken and you're about to record:

1. **Cut the existing CTA entirely.** Delete "if you enjoyed this, please subscribe."
2. **Write one sentence that resolves the hook's open loop.** "So that [hook element]? [Resolution]." (5 seconds)
3. **Write one sentence that looks forward.** "Next time, [specific next thing]." (5 seconds)
4. **Write one sentence that invites, not asks.** "If you want to be here for that, you know where the button is." (5 seconds)
5. **Read it aloud.** Does it sound like you? Does it flow from the script? If yes, record.

Fifteen seconds of ending. Three sentences. No robot. No obligation. Just resolution, forward motion, and a gentle door left open.
