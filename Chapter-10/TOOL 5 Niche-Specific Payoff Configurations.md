## TOOL 5: Niche-Specific Payoff Configurations

### *How endings adapt per content type. Add this column to your Niche Adaptation Blueprint (Chapter 9, Tool 2).*

---

### Educational / How-To — Payoff Signature

| Element | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Primary CTA Trigger** | Empowered Utility ("Next week you'll learn [specific skill]") |
| **Secondary Trigger** | Curiosity Bridge (tease the next lesson) |
| **Resolution Arc Length** | Short (15–20 sec). Land the lesson. Expand to universal application. Forward gaze = "try this today." |
| **Session Flow** | Callback to the Specific Promise in the hook. "I said you'd be able to [X] by the end. You can. Go do it." |
| **Pacing** | [Slow emphasis] on the final takeaway. [Pause 1.5s] before CTA. Warm, encouraging energy. No [Hard cut]. |
| **Voice Note** | Slightly warmer than baseline. Proud of the viewer. "You just learned something most people never figure out." |
| **Never** | "If you found this helpful, please subscribe." Guilt-tripping. Making it about the creator's needs. |

### Reaction / Entertainment — Payoff Signature

| Element | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Primary CTA Trigger** | Social Proof Nudge ("the comment section is where the real debate happens") |
| **Secondary Trigger** | Identity Reinforcement ("if you're the kind of person who [shares this reaction]...") |
| **Resolution Arc Length** | Very short (10–15 sec). One punchy summary line. Forward gaze = "next week we're reacting to [X]." |
| **Session Flow** | Callback to the moment of greatest surprise/outrage from the hook. "Remember when I said [hook claim]? Yeah. It's even worse than I thought." |
| **Pacing** | [Fast energy] on the forward gaze. [Hard cut] to end screen. [Pause 0.5s] for comedic timing before the final line. |
| **Voice Note** | High energy maintained. Slightly breathless. Like you're still processing what you just watched. |
| **Never** | Long reflective pauses. [Whisper]. [Slow down]. This niche ends on energy, not reflection. |

### Story-Driven / Vlog — Payoff Signature

| Element | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Primary CTA Trigger** | Emotional Debt Repayment ("send this to one person who needs to hear it") |
| **Secondary Trigger** | Identity Reinforcement (quiet, almost afterthought subscribe mention) |
| **Resolution Arc Length** | Longest (25–35 sec). Full three micro-beats. Let the emotion breathe. |
| **Session Flow** | Callback to the specific sensory detail or moment from the hook, now transformed. "That [detail from hook]? It looks different now." |
| **Pacing** | [Slow down] throughout. [Pause 2s] before the ask. [Whisper] or near-whisper for the final line. [Fade out energy]. Long silence at the end. |
| **Voice Note** | Quietest register. Most vulnerable. The voice is almost a murmur. Sincere. No performance. |
| **Never** | [Fast energy]. [Hard cut]. [Punch]. Exclamation points. "SMASH subscribe." Any energy that breaks the intimacy. |

---

### Review / Comparison — Payoff Signature

| Element | Configuration |
|---|---|
| **Primary CTA Trigger** | Curiosity Bridge ("Next month I'm testing [X] against [Y]") |
| **Secondary Trigger** | Empowered Utility ("Subscribe and the full comparison drops on your feed day one") |
| **Resolution Arc Length** | Short (15–20 sec). Restate the verdict cleanly. One universal principle. Forward gaze = next comparison. |
| **Session Flow** | Callback to the Contrarian Claim or Curiosity Gap from the hook. "So the [hook claim]? Confirmed. And now you know exactly what to look for." |
| **Pacing** | [Punch] on the final verdict word. [Pause 1s]. Clean, confident energy. [Text on screen] with the verdict summary. |
| **Voice Note** | Most authoritative register. Decisive. "Here's my call." No hedging. No "but you might prefer..." |
| **Never** | [Whisper]. [Slow down] for extended reflection. Emotional Debt Repayment (too soft for this niche). |
