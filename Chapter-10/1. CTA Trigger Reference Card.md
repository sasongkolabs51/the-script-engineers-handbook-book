## 1. CTA Trigger Reference Card

**Purpose:** A one‑page cheat sheet that replaces “please like and subscribe” with five psychologically rich triggers. Each includes a prompt instruction to paste into your payoff prompt.

| Trigger | What It Does | Example | Prompt Instruction |
|---------|--------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Identity Reinforcement** | Frames subscribing as an expression of a positive self‑image the video has just validated. | “If you’re the kind of person who refuses to buy a gadget without understanding the hidden compromises, you’re exactly why this channel exists. Hit subscribe and you’ll never miss a breakdown that treats you like the informed buyer you are.” | “Frame the CTA as an invitation to join a community defined by a shared value or identity that the video has just reinforced. The viewer should feel that subscribing is an expression of a positive trait they already possess. Avoid asking for a ‘favor.’” |
| **Curiosity Bridge** | Creates an information gap that only the next video can close, leveraging the Zeigarnik effect. | “I’ve been testing the three most recommended budget laptops for six weeks, and one of them had a keyboard failure so absurd I have to show you the footage. That’s coming next—if you’re subscribed, it’ll be waiting on your home screen.” | “End by previewing a specific, concrete detail or question that the next video will address. Make the viewer feel that subscribing is the only way to guarantee they’ll see the answer.” |
| **Social Proof Nudge** | Uses existing community behavior or credible metrics to signal worthiness, without begging. | “Thousands of creators watch these breakdowns every week—not because they agree with everything, but because the comment section has become one of the few places online where people debate like adults. If that sounds like your kind of space, hit subscribe and jump in.” | “Mention a specific, positive community behavior or a credible metric that signals the channel is worth joining. The tone must be observational, not pleading. No ‘please.’” |
| **Empowered Utility** | Positions the subscription as a tool for the viewer’s own growth or success, not a favour to the creator. | “Everything I just showed you about color grading took me three years of trial and error. The next video in this series breaks down a lighting setup that will save you at least an hour per shoot. Subscribe and that hour becomes yours.” | “Present subscribing as an act of self‑interest—a way for the viewer to gain more practical value with zero additional effort. Emphasise what they gain, never what you gain.” |
| **Emotional Debt Repayment** | After a vulnerable or profound story, asks the viewer to pay forward what they’ve received, usually through sharing. | “If this story resonated with you—if it made you think differently about something that matters—the most meaningful thing you can do is send it to one person who needs to hear it. That’s the only ask.” | “After a deeply personal or impactful section, make a single, soft ask that frames sharing as an act of generosity to someone else. The tone must be humble and sincere, never transactional. Do not mention subscribing unless it naturally extends the act of sharing.” |

**Combining triggers:** You can layer two triggers, e.g., an Identity Reinforcement into a Curiosity Bridge: “If you’re the kind of person who [identity], then the next video is for you—[specific tease]. Subscribe so you don’t miss it.”
