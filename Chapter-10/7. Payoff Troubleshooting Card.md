## 7. Payoff Troubleshooting Card

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| CTA sounds robotic or like a generic YouTuber. | Not using a trigger‑specific prompt; AI defaulted to “please subscribe.” | Replace “write a CTA” with the exact trigger instruction from the Reference Card. “Frame this as an Identity Reinforcement trigger: …” |
| Ending feels disconnected from the rest of the video. | Emotional Resolution Arc missing or too short; jumped straight from content to CTA. | Build the three micro‑beats (use worksheet). Even one sentence of emotional landing and a forward gaze can bridge the gap. |
| Session loop callback feels forced or cheesy. | Callback too literal; repeated hook verbatim. | Instead of repeating, echo the hook’s theme or language in a new context. “So that hidden spec issue we opened with? Here’s how you check for it every time.” |
| Voice drifts into formal presenter‑tone at the very end. | Voice Profile loses salience in longer scripts; AI defaults to “safe” closing language. | Add a “Voice Check” instruction at the end of your payoff prompt: “Before outputting, verify every sentence matches the Voice Profile. Replace any formal constructions with my colloquial fingerprint.” |
| Viewer retention drops steeply in the final 30 seconds. | Pacing too abrupt; no resolution arc, or CTA too long. | Ensure the resolution arc gives the viewer an exhale. Use [Slow down] and a [Pause 1.5s] before the forward gaze. Keep the CTA under 50 words. |
| Subscribe rate lower than expected, even with good views. | CTA trigger mismatched to niche or emotional tone. | Re‑evaluate the niche’s expected trigger. A personal vlog using Empowered Utility might feel transactional; switch to Emotional Debt Repayment or Identity Reinforcement. |
| Payoff feels like a different person wrote it. | You may have over‑adapted for the niche and lost your voice. | Re‑insert your Cross‑Niche Safeguard List (from Ch. 9) into the prompt. Ensure signature phrases, sentence architecture, and humor style remain. |
