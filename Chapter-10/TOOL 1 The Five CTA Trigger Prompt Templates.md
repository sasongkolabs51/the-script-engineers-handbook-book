## TOOL 1: The Five CTA Trigger Prompt Templates

### *One template per trigger. Each makes "asking" feel like "giving." Select based on your video's emotional journey and niche.*

---

### Trigger 1: Identity Reinforcement

**Psychology:** People act in alignment with who they believe they are. Connect the action (subscribing, sharing, commenting) to a positive identity the video just reinforced.

**When to use:** After a video that makes the viewer feel smart, discerning, brave, or part of a special group.

```
═══ CTA TRIGGER: IDENTITY REINFORCEMENT ═══

CONTEXT: The viewer just watched a video about [topic] that
reinforced their identity as [positive identity: e.g., "someone
who refuses to accept surface-level answers" / "a creator who
values craft over shortcuts" / "a person who questions before
they buy"].

Write a CTA (40–60 words) that:
- Names the positive identity the video just reinforced.
  ("If you're the kind of person who [specific behavior/trait]...")
- Frames subscribing/following as an EXPRESSION of that identity,
  not a favor to me.
- Makes the viewer feel that joining this channel/community is
  what someone like them NATURALLY does.
- Does NOT beg. Does NOT say "please." Does NOT say "if you
  enjoyed this."
- Ends with a forward-pointing statement about what they'll
  get by staying.

TONE: [your voice — e.g., "warm, slightly proud, like acknowledging
a friend's good taste"]
PACING: [e.g., "[Slow down] into the identity statement. [Pause 1s]
before the forward point."]

Generate TWO variants:
- Variant A: Direct and confident.
- Variant B: Softer, more invitational.
```

### Trigger 2: Curiosity Bridge

**Psychology:** The Zeigarnik effect—unclosed loops compel action. Create a specific information gap that only the next video can close.

**When to use:** When you have a clear next topic, a series, or a specific upcoming video that naturally follows.

```
═══ CTA TRIGGER: CURIOSITY BRIDGE ═══

CONTEXT: The viewer just watched a video about [topic]. The NEXT
video in my series/content plan is about [specific next topic].

THE SPECIFIC TEASE: [A concrete detail, question, or revelation
from the next video that creates genuine curiosity. Not "more
tips" but "the one setting that made my render times drop by
40%—and it's hidden in a menu nobody opens."]

Write a CTA (40–60 words) that:
- Transitions naturally from the current video's resolution.
- Introduces the specific tease as a forward-looking statement.
  ("Next week, I'm showing you [specific thing].")
- Includes ONE concrete detail that makes the tease irresistible.
  Not vague. Specific enough that the viewer thinks "I NEED
  to see that."
- Frames subscribing as the way to ensure they don't miss it.
  Not "subscribe for more" but "if you're subscribed, it'll
  be waiting on your home screen Tuesday morning."
- Does NOT oversell. Does NOT use exclamation points.

TONE: [your voice — e.g., "matter-of-fact, slightly conspiratorial,
like sharing what's coming next"]
PACING: [e.g., "[Pause 1s] before the tease. [Fast energy] on
the specific detail. Normal pace on the subscribe mention."]

Generate TWO variants:
- Variant A: Tease-first, then subscribe mention.
- Variant B: Subscribe mention woven into the tease naturally.
```

### Trigger 3: Social Proof Nudge

**Psychology:** Humans look to others' behavior when deciding. Signal that the community is worth joining—without begging for validation.

**When to use:** When your comment section is genuinely active, when you have a community identity, or when the video topic is debatable/discussable.

```
═══ CTA TRIGGER: SOCIAL PROOF NUDGE ═══

CONTEXT: The viewer just watched a video about [topic]. My
community/channel is known for [specific positive behavior:
e.g., "genuinely thoughtful debates in the comments" / "creators
who share their own experiments" / "people who disagree
respectfully and change each other's minds"].

THE PROOF POINT: [A specific, credible signal: "3,000 creators
watch these breakdowns weekly" / "the comment section on the
last video had 200+ responses and three people changed my mind"
/ "someone in the community built [specific thing] based on
the last framework I shared."]

Write a CTA (40–60 words) that:
- Mentions the community behavior or proof point naturally.
- Frames joining as entering a SPACE, not doing a favor.
  ("If that sounds like your kind of conversation...")
- Makes the viewer feel they'd be ADDING to the community,
  not just consuming.
- Does NOT sound needy. Does NOT say "it would really help
  me out." Does NOT guilt-trip.
- Invites, doesn't beg.

TONE: [your voice — e.g., "proud but not boastful, like
introducing a friend to a good dinner party"]
PACING: [e.g., "Normal pace. [Pause 0.5s] before the invitation.
Warm energy."]

Generate TWO variants:
- Variant A: Community-focused ("this space is...")
- Variant B: Viewer-focused ("you'd fit in because...")
```

### Trigger 4: Empowered Utility

**Psychology:** Position the action as pure self-interest. The viewer gains something concrete by subscribing/returning. Zero altruism required.

**When to use:** Educational content, tutorials, series with clear progressive value, "here's what you'll learn next" structures.

```
═══ CTA TRIGGER: EMPOWERED UTILITY ═══

CONTEXT: The viewer just learned [specific skill/insight] in
this video. The NEXT video/series installment will teach
[specific next skill/insight] that builds directly on this one.

THE UTILITY STATEMENT: [What the viewer GAINS by staying.
Specific and concrete. Not "more content" but "the exact
lighting setup that will save you 45 minutes per shoot" or
"the three-sentence email template that doubled my response rate."]

Write a CTA (40–60 words) that:
- States what the viewer just gained (briefly).
- States what they'll gain NEXT (specifically).
- Frames subscribing as the zero-effort way to get that next gain.
  ("Subscribe and [specific thing] is yours next Tuesday.")
- Emphasizes THEIR benefit, not mine.
- Does NOT say "support the channel." Does NOT make it about me.
- Makes the value proposition feel like a no-brainer.

TONE: [your voice — e.g., "practical, generous, like a colleague
saying 'hey, you'll want this next one'"]
PACING: [e.g., "[Slow emphasis] on the specific next gain.
[Pause 1s] before the subscribe mention. Clean, efficient."]

Generate TWO variants:
- Variant A: Gain-first ("Next week you'll get [X]...")
- Variant B: Contrast-first ("This took me 3 years. The next
  video gives it to you in 12 minutes...")
```

### Trigger 5: Emotional Debt Repayment

**Psychology:** After a genuine emotional experience, the viewer feels moved. Channel that feeling into generosity toward others (sharing), not obligation toward you.

**When to use:** After vulnerable stories, personal essays, transformative insights, or any video that gave the viewer a genuine emotional experience.

```
═══ CTA TRIGGER: EMOTIONAL DEBT REPAYMENT ═══

CONTEXT: The viewer just experienced [describe the emotional
journey: e.g., "a vulnerable story about professional failure
and recovery" / "a reframing that challenged something they'd
believed for years" / "a moment of catharsis around a shared
struggle"].

THE FORWARD ASK: [Not "subscribe." Instead: "send this to one
person who needs to hear it" / "share this with someone who's
in the middle of [the struggle]" / "tell one person about
[the insight] this week."]

Write a CTA (40–60 words) that:
- Acknowledges the emotional weight of what was just shared.
  Briefly. One sentence. No performance.
- Makes a SINGLE, soft ask: share this with someone who needs it.
- Frames sharing as an act of GENEROSITY to another person,
  not support for the channel.
- Is humble. Sincere. Slightly vulnerable. Never transactional.
- Does NOT say "like and subscribe." Does NOT mention the
  algorithm. Does NOT say "it really helps the channel."
- May optionally add a quiet subscribe mention at the very end,
  almost as an afterthought: "And if you want to be here for
  the next one, you know where the button is."

TONE: [your voice — e.g., "quiet, sincere, slightly raw. Like
the last sentence of a letter to a friend."]
PACING: [e.g., "[Slow down] throughout. [Pause 2s] before the
ask. [Whisper] or near-whisper for the final line. [Fade out
energy]."]

Generate TWO variants:
- Variant A: Share-focused (the primary ask is sending to someone).
- Variant B: Reflective (the primary ask is sitting with the
  feeling; subscribe is a gentle footnote).
```

### Trigger Selection Quick Guide

| Video Type / Emotional Journey | Primary Trigger | Secondary Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Educational / "you just learned a skill" | Empowered Utility | Curiosity Bridge |
| Review / "you just got a verdict" | Curiosity Bridge | Empowered Utility |
| Personal story / "you just felt something" | Emotional Debt Repayment | Identity Reinforcement |
| Reaction / "we just experienced that together" | Social Proof Nudge | Identity Reinforcement |
| Contrarian / "your assumptions were challenged" | Identity Reinforcement | Curiosity Bridge |
| Series installment / "there's a clear next step" | Curiosity Bridge | Empowered Utility |
| Vulnerable essay / "I shared something real" | Emotional Debt Repayment | Social Proof Nudge |
