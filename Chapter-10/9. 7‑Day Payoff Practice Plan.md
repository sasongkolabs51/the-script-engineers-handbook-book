## 9. 7‑Day Payoff Practice Plan

**Day 1 – Learn the Triggers**  
- [ ] Study the CTA Trigger Reference Card.  
- [ ] For each trigger, find an example from a creator you respect (or write one yourself).  
- [ ] Note which trigger feels most natural for your primary niche.

**Day 2 – Build Emotional Resolution Arcs**  
- [ ] Take your last three published scripts. For each, write a missing Emotional Resolution Arc (even if you didn’t originally have one).  
- [ ] Use the worksheet to practice the three micro‑beats.  
- [ ] Notice how much more complete the scripts feel.

**Day 3 – Session Flow Mapping**  
- [ ] For your next three video ideas, fill out the Session Flow Map Template. Design the hook and its corresponding payoff callback before writing the full script.  
- [ ] Ensure each hook contains a clear curiosity gap or promise that the payoff can close.

**Day 4 – First Payoff Generation**  
- [ ] Take a completed script. Run the CTA & Payoff Generator Prompt.  
- [ ] Score the three variants with the Scoring Sheet. Pick the best and refine if needed.  
- [ ] Record yourself reading the winning ending aloud. Check pacing and voice.

**Day 5 – Niche‑Specific Payoff Tuning**  
- [ ] Add a “Payoff Signature” column to your Niche Adaptation Blueprint (or create a separate add‑on).  
- [ ] Define the default trigger, resolution arc length, session flow expectation, and pacing tokens for your primary niche.  
- [ ] If you create in a secondary niche, build its payoff signature today as well.

**Day 6 – Test and Compare**  
- [ ] If possible, publish a video with the new payoff architecture. Compare end‑of‑video retention and subscribe rate with a recent video that used a generic CTA.  
- [ ] Review comments for signs of satisfaction (“Can’t wait for the next one!”) or disconnection.

**Day 7 – Build a Payoff Prompt Library**  
- [ ] Save your CTA & Payoff Generator Prompt as a template.  
- [ ] Store your Emotional Resolution Arc Builder and Session Flow Map as regular steps in your Layer 3+ workflow.  
- [ ] Write a one‑sentence summary: “The biggest change in my video endings now is ________.”
