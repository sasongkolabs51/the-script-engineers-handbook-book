## TOOL 10: The 30-Day Identity Integration Plan

### *Because reading a chapter about identity doesn't change your identity. Living it for 30 days does.*

---

### Week 1: The Excavation (Days 1–7)

**Theme:** Get the guilt out. Name it. Reframe it. Build the evidence base.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Complete Tool 1 (Zero-Sum Belief Audit). All thoughts. No censoring. | 45 min | ☐ |
| 2 | Complete Tool 2 (Identity Reframe Canvas). Fill in all three layers with specifics. | 30 min | ☐ |
| 3 | Complete Tool 3 (What I Actually Did Inventory) on your best script. | 30 min | ☐ |
| 4 | Complete Tool 5 (Script Audit with New Eyes) on your most guilt-laden script. | 30 min | ☐ |
| 5 | Complete Tool 6 (Audience Trust Inventory). Read your old comments. | 25 min | ☐ |
| 6 | Write your Creator Manifesto (Tool 4). Slowly. In your own words. Handwritten if possible. | 45 min | ☐ |
| 7 | **Rest day.** Re-read your manifesto once. Out loud. Then close the notebook. Go make something unrelated. Cook. Walk. Let it settle. | 10 min | ☐ |

---

### Week 2: The Practice (Days 8–14)

**Theme:** Use the three-layer model in real production. Feel the difference.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8 | Before writing any prompt, complete Tool 7 (Three-Layer Decision Checklist). Confirm your Visionary and Craftsman decisions BEFORE opening the AI tool. | 5 min | ☐ |
| 9 | Write a script using Prompt Template A (Tool 9). Notice how it feels to prompt FROM your identity rather than hoping the AI guesses it. | 50 min | ☐ |
| 10 | Edit the script. Use Tool 7's post-generation checks. Note: did you feel less guilt during editing? More? Same? Write one sentence about it. | Variable | ☐ |
| 11 | Use Prompt Template B (Give Me Options) for one section. Practice the Craftsman act of *selecting* rather than *generating*. Notice: selection IS creative work. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 12 | Use Prompt Template C (Reshape This) on a draft. Make 5 specific Craftsman decisions. Watch the AI execute them. Notice: you're directing. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 13 | Use Prompt Template D (Voice Calibration) to lock in your voice spec. Save it as a reusable block. This is YOUR voice, specified by YOU. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 14 | **Weekly reflection:** Read your manifesto. Has any line become more true this week? Has any guilt thought lost power? Write one sentence about the shift. | 10 min | ☐ |

---

### Week 3: The Stress Test (Days 15–21)

**Theme:** Encounter triggers. Practice the circuit breaker. Prove the identity holds under pressure.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15 | Produce a full script using the three-layer workflow. Start to finish. No template browsing. No guilt spirals. Just: Vision → Craft → Engineer → Shape. | 60 min | ☐ |
| 16 | Publish something. Hit the button. Notice the guilt. Run the 90-Second Protocol (Tool 8). Publish anyway. | 5 min + publish | ☐ |
| 17 | Read a comment or DM (real or imagined) that triggers the "they'll know it's AI" fear. Sit with it for 60 seconds. Then re-read your Audience Trust Inventory. | 10 min | ☐ |
| 18 | Watch a "how I write my scripts" video from a creator who does it "manually." Notice the comparison impulse. Name it: "That's the zero-sum thought." Then notice: they use tools too (thesaurus, outlines, editors, feedback). The spectrum is wider than you think. | 15 min | ☐ |
| 19 | Teach the Three-Layered model to someone. A friend. A community post. A voice memo. Explain it in your own words. Teaching cements identity. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 20 | Deliberately generate a script section that's *better* than what you'd type yourself. Sit with the discomfort. Then ask: "Did I design the structure that produced this? Did I select it? Did I shape it?" Yes. It's yours. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 21 | **Weekly reflection:** Update your manifesto if needed. Add a line. Cross out a line. It's a living document. You're allowed to revise your identity as it solidifies. | 15 min | ☐ |

---

### Week 4: The Integration (Days 22–30)

**Theme:** Make this your default. The new normal. No more "AI guilt" as a recurring theme.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 22–27 | Normal production. Every session starts with Tool 7 (checklist). Every prompt uses the three-layer structure. Every edit is a Craftsman decision you own. | 5 min pre-work | ☐ |
| 28 | Re-read your Zero-Sum Belief Audit (Tool 1). For each guilt thought, rate its current power: 1 (barely a whisper) to 10 (still stops me cold). Compare to Day 1. | 15 min | ☐ |
| 29 | Write a short "letter to the creator I was 30 days ago." The one who almost didn't upload. The one who felt the watermark on the pixels. What do you know now? | 20 min | ☐ |
| 30 | Read your manifesto aloud. Sign it again. Date it. Commit to the next 30 days, and the 30 after that. The identity isn't a destination. It's a practice. | 10 min | ☐ |

---

### The Ongoing Practice (After Day 30)

You don't need a structured plan forever. You need three small habits:

> **Daily (2 minutes):** Before prompting, glance at Tool 7. Confirm your Visionary and Craftsman decisions. Then let the AI do its Engineer job.
>
> **When guilt flares (90 seconds):** Run the Circuit Breaker protocol. Name the thought. Read the card. Say the mantra. Keep working.
>
> **Monthly (15 minutes):** Re-read your manifesto. Re-read your "What I Actually Did" inventory. Add new evidence. The file grows. The guilt shrinks.
