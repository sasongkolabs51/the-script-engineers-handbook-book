## TOOL 1: The Zero-Sum Belief Audit

### *Get every guilt thought out of the fog and onto paper. Then look it in the eye and rewrite it.*

**Instructions:** Draw a line down the middle of a page (or use the table below). On the left, write every thought you've ever had that frames AI as a threat to your authenticity, your legitimacy, your "realness" as a creator. Don't censor. Don't judge. Write the ugly ones. The ones you'd never say out loud. The ones that make you feel small.

Then, on the right, rewrite each one using the Three-Layered model. Not with toxic positivity. Not with "just think positive!" But with *structural accuracy*. What's actually true about your role in the creative process?

### The Belief Audit Follow-Up Rule

For the next 30 days, every time a guilt thought from your left column pops up mid-work:

1. **Name it.** ("Ah, there's #4 again. The 'they'll think I'm lazy' thought.")
2. **Read the reframe.** (Glance at your right column.)
3. **Keep working.** Don't argue with the thought. Don't suppress it. Just name it, reframe it, and continue.

The thought will come back. Probably daily for the first week. Then weekly. Then monthly. Each time you name-and-reframe without stopping, the neural pathway weakens. This isn't magic. It's repetition.
