## TOOL 9: AI Prompt Templates — Prompting FROM the Three-Layer Identity

### *These prompts position you explicitly as Visionary and Craftsman. The AI is framed as your Engineer. The structure makes the identity real in every interaction.*

---

### Prompt Template A: The "I'm the Director" Framework

*(Use this as your default prompt structure. It encodes the three layers into every interaction.)*

```
═══ MY VISION (Layer 1 — non-negotiable) ═══

TOPIC: [Your chosen topic]
ANGLE: [Your specific framing — why this angle, why now]
EMOTIONAL TARGET: [What the viewer should feel by the end]
AUDIENCE INSIGHT: [What you know about your viewers that shapes this script]
PERSONAL ELEMENT: [Your story, your experience, your vulnerability beat]
WHAT I REFUSE TO COMPROMISE: [Your values line — what this script must NOT do]

═══ MY CRAFT (Layer 2 — my structural design) ═══

STRUCTURE: [Your beat-by-beat architecture — hook type, section order,
escalation logic, payoff placement]
PACING: [Where energy rises, falls, pauses]
TONE: [Your specific voice calibration — not "casual" but "like I'm
explaining this to my friend Dave over bad coffee"]
HUMOR: [Your style: dry / self-deprecating / absurd / none]
REJECTIONS: [What I do NOT want: no "let's dive in," no exclamation
points in the CTA, no rhetorical questions in the hook, etc.]

═══ YOUR JOB (Layer 3 — execution only) ═══

You are my Engineer. Your role is to generate raw prose that follows
MY structure, MY tone, and MY emotional design. You do not choose
the angle. You do not decide the pacing. You do not inject your own
"engagement" ideas. You execute my blueprint with precision and
produce clean, varied prose that I will then shape further.

Generate the script following the structure above. Produce [1 / 2 / 3]
variation(s) of the hook for me to select from. Flag any point where
my instructions are ambiguous so I can clarify, rather than guessing.

After the script, add a brief "Engineer's Note" listing any points
where you had to make a judgment call, so I can review those
Craftsman-level decisions myself.
```

### Prompt Template B: The "Give Me Options, I'll Decide" Framework

*(Use when you want the AI to generate variations within YOUR constraints, so you can exercise Craftsman-level selection.)*

```
═══ CONTEXT ═══
I'm writing a [video type] about [topic] for [audience].
My angle: [specific framing].
My voice: [2–3 sentence description of how you actually sound].

═══ THE BEAT I NEED ═══
I need [specific section: e.g., "the hook" / "the transition between
Section 2 and Section 3" / "the CTA"].

The structural job this beat must do: [e.g., "create a curiosity gap
using a specific number" / "escalate stakes by connecting to the
viewer's identity" / "release tension with a dry joke before the
final section"].

═══ YOUR JOB ═══
Generate THREE distinct variations of this beat. Each must fulfill
the structural job above. Vary the approach:
- Variation 1: [approach A, e.g., "lead with the number"]
- Variation 2: [approach B, e.g., "lead with the personal admission"]
- Variation 3: [approach C, e.g., "lead with the contrarian claim"]

Constraints for ALL variations:
- Max [X] words
- Tone: [your specific calibration]
- Must include: [specific element you want]
- Must NOT include: [specific elements you reject]

═══ MY ROLE ═══
I will select one variation and reshape it. You are generating
raw material for my Craftsman judgment. Do not add a "best option"
recommendation. That's my decision.
```

### Prompt Template C: The "Reshape This" Framework

*(Use when you have a draft—AI-generated or your own—and need the AI to execute specific Craftsman-level edits you've already decided on.)*

```
═══ THE DRAFT ═══
[Paste your current draft or section here]

═══ MY CRAFTSMAN DECISIONS (already made — do not override) ═══
I have decided the following edits. Execute them precisely:

1. [e.g., "Cut the second paragraph entirely. It's filler."]
2. [e.g., "Move the personal anecdote from paragraph 4 to paragraph 1."]
3. [e.g., "Rewrite the hook to start with the specific number '43'
   instead of the rhetorical question."]
4. [e.g., "Shorten every sentence in Section 2 to under 15 words."]
5. [e.g., "Replace the CTA with one that references the pain point
   from the opening. No 'subscribe' mention. Frame it as the viewer's
   next logical step."]

═══ CONSTRAINTS ═══
- Do NOT change the overall structure or section order (except where
  I've explicitly instructed a move).
- Do NOT add new content I haven't asked for.
- Do NOT "improve" my tone or make it "more engaging." My tone
  decisions are final.
- Maintain my voice: [brief description].

═══ OUTPUT ═══
Return the reshaped draft. Then list any sentences where you were
unsure how to execute my instruction, so I can make the final call.
```

### Prompt Template D: The "Voice Calibration" Prompt

*(Use to lock in your specific voice at the Craftsman layer, so the Engineer output sounds like you from the first draft.)*

```
═══ VOICE DNA (my Craftsman-layer specification) ═══

I sound like: [2–3 sentences describing your actual speaking voice.
Not "casual and fun." Be specific: "I talk like a slightly tired
friend who's had two coffees and is explaining something they find
genuinely fascinating but also slightly absurd. I use 'look' and
'here's the thing' as pivots. I trail off mid-thought sometimes.
I never say 'amazing' or 'incredible.' I say 'weird' and 'honest'
and 'kind of brutal.'"]

Sentence rhythm: [e.g., "I alternate between long, winding sentences
(18–22 words) and short punches (4–7 words). I never do three long
sentences in a row. I use fragments for emphasis. Like this."]

Vocabulary boundaries:
- Words I USE: [list 5–10]
- Words I NEVER use: [list 5–10]
- Phrases that are MINE: [list 2–3 signature phrases]

Emotional register: [e.g., "I'm honest but not performative. I'll
admit failure without making it a whole production. My vulnerability
is dry, not tearful. I'd say 'yeah, that was stupid' not 'I want
to take you on a journey through my pain.'"]

═══ INSTRUCTION ═══
Apply this Voice DNA to [the following section / the entire script].
The structure and content are already decided. You are calibrating
the ENGINEER-layer prose to match my CRAFTSMAN-layer voice spec.
Do not change what is said. Change only how it sounds.
```
