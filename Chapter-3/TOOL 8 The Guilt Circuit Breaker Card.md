### The 90-Second Guilt Protocol

When the spiral hits, do this. Not "think positive." Do this specific sequence:

**Step 1 (10 seconds):** Name the thought. "I'm having the 'I'm a fraud' thought." Don't argue with it. Just label it.

**Step 2 (20 seconds):** Read the Circuit Breaker Card. All six questions. Answer them honestly.

**Step 3 (20 seconds):** Open your "What I Actually Did" Inventory (Tool 3) or your Script Audit (Tool 5). Read one specific example of a Visionary or Craftsman decision you made.

**Step 4 (20 seconds):** Read your mantra aloud. Out loud. Where your cat can hear you. Where the walls can hear you. Say it like you mean it.

**Step 5 (20 seconds):** Do one small creative action. Edit one sentence. Adjust one transition. Make one Craftsman-level decision. Prove to yourself, in real time, that you are shaping this work.

**Then:** Hit publish. Or keep editing. Or go to bed. But do NOT delete the script out of guilt. Do NOT abandon the workflow. Do NOT open a blank document and try to "prove" you can write it "for real." That's the zero-sum voice talking. You don't owe it obedience.
