## TOOL 7: The Three-Layer Decision Checklist (Daily Operational Tool)

### *Use this every time you sit down to create. 60 seconds. Keeps you grounded in your role.*

---

**Before I open my AI tool, I confirm:**

| Layer | My Job Today | Done? |
|---|---|---|
| **VISIONARY** | I have decided: the topic is ___________, the angle is ___________, the emotional target is ___________, and this matters because ___________. | ☐ |
| **CRAFTSMAN** | I have decided: the structure will be ___________, the hook type will be ___________, the pacing will ___________, and the tone will be ___________. | ☐ |
| **ENGINEER** | I will instruct the AI to: generate raw material following MY structure, produce variations for ME to select, and handle execution tasks I've specified. | ☐ |

**The AI's boundary today:**

> The AI will generate _________. I will decide _________. I will shape _________. I will reject _________.

---

**After the AI generates output, I confirm:**

| Check | Question | Answer |
|---|---|---|
| Visionary check | Does this output serve MY chosen angle and emotional target? | ☐ Yes ☐ Needs redirect |
| Craftsman check | Does the structure, pacing, and tone match MY design? | ☐ Yes ☐ Needs reshaping |
| Voice check | Does this sound like ME? Would I say this to a friend? | ☐ Yes ☐ Needs editing |
| Rejection check | Is there anything here I'd never say? Anything that violates my values or style? | ☐ Clean ☐ Cut: _________ |
| Final judgment | Am I proud to put my name on this? | ☐ Yes ☐ Not yet |

---

**The rule:** If the Visionary or Craftsman check fails, the problem isn't the AI. The problem is my prompt didn't specify my vision/craft clearly enough. I go back and re-engineer the instruction. I don't blame the tool. I don't feel guilty. I adjust the blueprint.
