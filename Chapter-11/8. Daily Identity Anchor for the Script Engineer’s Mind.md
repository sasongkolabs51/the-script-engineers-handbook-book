## 8. Daily Identity Anchor for the Script Engineer’s Mind

Read this aloud at the start of your creative session, or whenever you feel the old doubts creeping back.

> I am not a prompt user. I am a story‑orchestrator.  
> My system is not a crutch; it is my instrument, and I practice it weekly.  
> When output degrades, I don’t panic—I isolate, compare, adjust, and document.  
> My Engineering Log turns every script, every failure, every insight into an asset that compounds.  
> My identity is not threatened by a broken layer; it is proven by my ability to fix it.  
> I am no longer chasing secrets; I am building capability, one deliberate rep at a time.  
> Today, I will create with the quiet confidence of someone who knows their tools, trusts their taste, and can engineer their way through any creative block.  
> I am a Script Engineer. This is my practice. This is my craft. This is how I make work that matters.
