## 9. 7‑Day Installation Plan for the Script Engineer’s Mind

**Day 1 – Create Your Practice Space**  
- [ ] Set a recurring weekly appointment for your 60‑minute Practice Ritual. Guard it.  
- [ ] Create your Engineering Log document (Notion, Google Doc, or notebook) with the four sections.  
- [ ] Read the Daily Identity Anchor aloud.

**Day 2 – First Practice Session**  
- [ ] Run the full Weekly Practice Ritual (Section 1) for the first time. Don’t aim for perfection; just do the drills.  
- [ ] Log your first insight in the Breakthrough Tracker.

**Day 3 – Populate the Chain Archive**  
- [ ] Go back through your last 3–5 published videos and fill in the Chain Archive with their configurations and ratings.  
- [ ] Add any notable insights or “I wish I’d done X” notes.

**Day 4 – Simulate a Reset**  
- [ ] Deliberately break a layer (e.g., remove a guardrail or change your voice profile drastically) in a test prompt. Then run the Reset Protocol to diagnose and fix it.  
- [ ] Document the “failure” and your fix in the Failure Autopsy section. This teaches you the protocol in a low‑stakes way.

**Day 5 – Monthly Skill Audit (even if early)**  
- [ ] Complete the Monthly Skill Audit (Section 4) for your work so far. Set a 30‑day focus.  
- [ ] Update your Progress Dashboard with current baselines.

**Day 6 – Identity Reflection**  
- [ ] Re‑read your Creator Manifesto from Chapter 3. Does it still feel true? Make any updates.  
- [ ] Record a voice memo of yourself explaining the Script Engineer’s Mind to an imaginary new creator. Teaching reinforces identity.

**Day 7 – Lock In the Rhythms**  
- [ ] Transfer the Daily, Weekly, Monthly, and Quarterly rhythms to your calendar as recurring events.  
- [ ] Write a single commitment: “I will maintain my Engineering Log and weekly practice for the next 90 days, no matter what.”  
- [ ] Read the Daily Identity Anchor one more time and then begin your work.
