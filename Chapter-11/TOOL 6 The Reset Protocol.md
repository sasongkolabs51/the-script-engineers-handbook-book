### Step 3: Adjust the Affected Layer Only (10–15 min)

| If the broken layer is... | The fix is... |
|---|---|
| **Layer 1 (Ideation)** | Re-examine your beat script. Has the topic shifted? Is the framework still appropriate? Re-run with more specific angle/emotional outcome. |
| **Layer 2 (Skeleton)** | Check guardrails. Are they still in the prompt? Is the beat script complete? Add the Summary Prohibition Guard if summary mode crept in. |
| **Layer 3 (Pacing)** | Check token formatting. Are tokens in [SQUARE BRACKETS]? Is the tempo map still in the prompt? Add explicit placement examples. |
| **Layer 4 (Voice)** | Re-run the Voice Calibration Loop (Ch. 7, Tool 5). Has your voice evolved? Update the profile. Check for banned phrases that slipped through. |
| **Payoff** | Check the CTA trigger instruction. Is it specific? Is the Emotional Resolution Arc present? Re-run the CTA Generator with explicit trigger instructions. |
| **AI model update** | The model changed how it interprets your prompts. Tighten instructions. Add more explicit examples. Reduce ambiguity. Document what changed. |

### The Emotional Protocol (read this when you feel the panic)

> **This is not a crisis. This is maintenance.**
>
> A system that never needs recalibration is a fantasy. A system that can be diagnosed and repaired in 30 minutes is a professional asset.
>
> I am not "losing my touch." I am an engineer performing routine maintenance. The system works. One layer needs adjustment. I will adjust it. And I will be back to full performance within the hour.
>
> The old impulse says: "Maybe you need a new template. Maybe this doesn't work." That's the Secret-Seeker talking. I don't chase new templates. I diagnose and repair. That's what engineers do.
>
> My identity is not threatened by a poorly performing layer. It is DEMONSTRATED by my ability to identify and fix it.
