## 6. Before/After Structural Prompt Comparison Card

**Purpose:** A quick side‑by‑side reference to remind you what you’re leaving behind and what you’re moving toward.

| Old Way (Detail Fallacy) | New Way (Structural Prompting) |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------|
| "Write a casual intro about productivity apps failing after 3 weeks. Make it relatable and fun." | "Write an intro with a Hook: a specific embarrassing personal stat about apps I’ve abandoned. Then Stakes: why this pattern secretly costs me hours every week. End with a Tension Loop: a question that won’t be answered until the final section." |
| "Be more engaging." | "Open with a surprising contradiction. Escalate to a personal consequence within 8 seconds. Leave a clear unanswered question." |
| "Sound natural." | "Use contractions, fragments, and direct address. Read each line aloud—if it feels like a written essay, rewrite it." |
| "Add some humor." | "Include one self‑deprecating observation about a failure that ties to the topic, delivered in a short, dry sentence." |
