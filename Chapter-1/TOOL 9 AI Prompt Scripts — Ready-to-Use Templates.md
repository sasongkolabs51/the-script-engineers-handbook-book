## TOOL 9: AI Prompt Scripts — Ready-to-Use Templates

### *Three complete prompts you can adapt immediately. Each one embodies structural prompting. No adjectives-as-instructions. All architecture.*

### Prompt Script A: The "Personal Story" Explainer Video

*(Use for: videos where you share a lesson learned, a mistake, or a transformation.)*

```
You are writing a YouTube script for my channel about [niche]. My audience
is [specific viewer description: e.g., "freelance designers aged 25–40 who
undercharge and feel guilty about it"].

TOPIC: [e.g., "The pricing mistake that cost me $18K in one quarter"]

TARGET LENGTH: ~1,400 words (9–11 minutes spoken)

STRUCTURE — follow exactly:

[HOOK — 0:00 to 0:12]
Open mid-story. I'm [specific moment: e.g., "staring at an invoice I sent
three months ago"]. Include the specific dollar amount or number. One
sentence fragment for punch. Do NOT greet the audience. Do NOT say "hey
guys." Drop the viewer into the scene.

[STAKES — 0:12 to 0:30]
Zoom out. This isn't just my problem. Name the viewer's version of this
pain in "you" language. One sentence that makes them feel seen: "You've
probably [specific relatable behavior]." Then escalate: "And the cost
isn't just money. It's [deeper consequence]."

[TENSION LOOP #1 — 0:30 to 0:40]
"I eventually figured out what I was doing wrong. And it wasn't [obvious
answer]. It was [tease the real answer without fully stating it]. I'll
show you exactly what that looks like in about four minutes. But first,
you need to understand why [setup for Section 1]."

[SECTION 1: The Setup / Context — 0:40 to 3:00]
Tell the story of how I got into this situation. 3–4 short paragraphs.
End with a pivot: "And that's when [inciting incident]."

[SECTION 2: The Realization — 3:00 to 5:30]
The "aha" moment. What I discovered. Include one concrete example or
analogy: [specify, e.g., "It's like a restaurant that prices its menu
based on how much the chef enjoys cooking, not what the diner values"].
Escalate: "And here's the part that made me feel stupid..."

[TENSION LOOP #2 — 5:30 to 5:45]
"Now, knowing this changed everything. But there's one specific situation
where this advice completely falls apart. And if you're in that situation
right now, everything I just said will make things worse. Here's how to
tell..."

[SECTION 3: The Exception / Nuance — 5:45 to 7:30]
Address the exception. Resolve Tension Loop #2. Give the viewer a clear
"if X, then Y" decision framework.

[PAYOFF — 7:30 to 8:30]
Close Tension Loop #1 explicitly. State the lesson in one clean sentence.
Reference the hook detail: "That $18K invoice? I re-sent it with one
changed line. They paid in full within 48 hours."

[CTA — 8:30 to 9:00]
Connect to the stakes: "If you're still [pain from stakes section], the
smallest thing you can do today is [one specific action]. And if you want
the full framework I use for [related topic], the video on screen right
now breaks it down step by step."

VOICE RULES:
- Contractions always. Write like I talk to a friend.
- Max sentence length: 20 words. Follow any 18+ word sentence with one
  under 8 words.
- One dry joke after the stakes beat. Observational, not punny.
- Use "you" at least 6 times in the first 90 seconds.
- Banned phrases: "In today's video," "Let's dive in," "Without further
  ado," "comprehensive," "delve," "landscape."

OUTPUT: Full script with [SECTION HEADERS] and (timestamps). End with a
3-bullet Structural Integrity Check confirming: hook has a specific number,
stakes use "you" language, and both tension loops are opened and resolved.
```

### Prompt Script B: The "List / Breakdown" Educational Video

*(Use for: "5 reasons why...", "3 mistakes...", "The complete guide to..." formats.)*

```
You are writing a YouTube script for [channel/niche]. Audience: [viewer
description].

TOPIC: [e.g., "5 reasons your morning routine collapses by Wednesday"]

TARGET LENGTH: ~1,600 words (10–12 minutes)

STRUCTURE:

[HOOK — 0:00 to 0:10]
Do NOT say "Here are 5 reasons." Instead, open with the most counter-
intuitive reason as a mini-story: "Reason number three on this list is
the one nobody talks about, and it's the reason I [specific personal
failure]. But we'll get there. First, you need to understand why reasons
one and two are quietly sabotaging you before you even get out of bed."

[STAKES — 0:10 to 0:25]
"If you've tried [common solution] and it lasted [short timeframe], this
isn't a discipline problem. It's a design problem. And by the end of this
video, you'll have [specific outcome]."

[TENSION LOOP — 0:25 to 0:35]
"I'm going to count down from 5 to 1. But here's the catch: reason #1
isn't what you expect. And it only makes sense after you understand #3.
So stick with me."

[REASON 5 — brief, 60 seconds]
[REASON 4 — brief, 60 seconds]
[REASON 3 — the "nobody talks about" one, 90 seconds, include a personal
anecdote]
[REASON 2 — 75 seconds, escalate urgency]
[REASON 1 — the reframe / surprise, 90 seconds, resolve the tension loop]

[PAYOFF — 30 seconds]
One-sentence summary. Reference the hook story.

[CTA — 15 seconds]
Tie to stakes. "If your routine keeps dying by Wednesday, [one action].
And the video on screen shows you [related deeper topic]."

VOICE RULES:
[same as Prompt Script A — adjust to your voice]

OUTPUT: Full script with headers, timestamps, and Structural Integrity Check.
```

### Prompt Script C: The "Hot Take / Opinion" Video

*(Use for: contrarian takes, industry commentary, "unpopular opinion" content.)*

```
You are writing a YouTube script for [channel/niche]. Audience: [viewer
description].

TOPIC / TAKE: [e.g., "Hustle culture isn't toxic. Your implementation of
it is. And here's the difference nobody wants to make."]

TARGET LENGTH: ~1,200 words (8–9 minutes)

STRUCTURE:

[HOOK — 0:00 to 0:10]
State the contrarian position in one blunt sentence. No softening. No
"some people might say." Just: "[Hot take stated plainly]." Then
immediately: "And before you click away, let me show you the data that
changed my mind. Because I used to believe the opposite."

[STAKES — 0:10 to 0:30]
"Here's why this matters to you specifically: if you've been [common
behavior driven by the 'wrong' belief], you're not lazy / broken /
undisciplined. You've been following advice that was never designed for
[your specific situation]. And the cost is [specific consequence]."

[TENSION LOOP — 0:30 to 0:40]
"I'm going to make this argument in three parts. But part two is going
to make you uncomfortable, because it means [implication the viewer
doesn't want to accept]. I'll get there. First, the setup."

[PART 1: Steelman the opposing view — 2 minutes]
Present the common belief fairly. "Here's what people mean when they say
[common belief]. And they're not wrong that [valid point]."

[PART 2: The reframe / uncomfortable truth — 3 minutes]
The pivot. "But here's what that framing misses..." Include one specific
example, case study, or data point. This is where the viewer's identity
is gently challenged. Use "I" statements to soften: "I had to accept
that I..."

[PART 3: The actionable reframe — 2 minutes]
"So what do you actually DO with this? Three specific shifts: [1, 2, 3]."
Keep each to 2–3 sentences. Concrete. No fluff.

[PAYOFF — 20 seconds]
Circle back to the hook. "So no, hustle culture isn't the enemy. But
[your specific reframe] is the difference between burning out and
building something. And that's not toxic. That's just honest."

[CTA — 15 seconds]
"If this ruffled some feathers, good. Tell me in the comments where you
think I'm wrong. And if you want the practical version of [Part 3],
the video on screen walks through it in real time."

VOICE RULES:
[same as Prompt Script A — adjust to your voice]

OUTPUT: Full script with headers, timestamps, and Structural Integrity Check.
```
