## TOOL 5: The Structural Prompt Builder (Fill-In Template)

### *Your go-to template for writing prompts that engineer retention instead of describing vibes.*

Copy this. Fill in the brackets. Paste into your AI tool. Adjust as needed for your specific video.

---

```
ROLE: You are a YouTube scriptwriter specializing in [your niche/channel topic].
You write for an audience of [describe viewer: age range, knowledge level,
what they're struggling with, what they've already tried].

VIDEO TOPIC: [Specific topic — not "productivity" but "why the 3-week
productivity app cycle keeps repeating"]

TOTAL TARGET LENGTH: [e.g., 8–10 minutes / ~1,200–1,500 words]

--- STRUCTURAL ARCHITECTURE (follow this beat-by-beat) ---

HOOK (first 15 seconds / ~40 words):
- Open with [a specific number / a personal confession / a contradiction
  of common belief / a "you're doing X wrong" statement].
- The hook must contain ONE concrete, surprising detail that makes the
  viewer think "wait, what?"
- Do NOT open with a rhetorical question. Do NOT open with "Have you
  ever..." Do NOT state the video topic directly.

STAKES (next 15–20 seconds / ~50 words):
- Immediately make the topic feel personally urgent to the viewer.
- Use "you" language. Reference a specific pain point: [describe the
  pain — e.g., "wasting $200/year on apps you abandon"].
- Include one short personal admission or vulnerability beat:
  [your real experience in 1–2 sentences].

TENSION LOOP #1 (end of intro / ~20 words):
- Pose a specific question or tease a specific revelation that will NOT
  be answered until [timestamp / section name].
- Format: "But the real reason isn't [obvious answer]. It's something
  I didn't figure out until [specific moment]. And it changes everything
  about how you [relevant action]."

BODY SECTION 1 — [Name this section]:
- Deliver [first key insight / argument / story].
- End this section with a mini-cliffhanger or pivot: "But here's where
  it gets uncomfortable..."

BODY SECTION 2 — [Name this section]:
- Deliver [second key insight]. Escalate stakes: why does this matter
  MORE than the viewer initially thought?
- Include one concrete example, case study, or analogy: [specify].

BODY SECTION 3 — [Name this section]:
- Deliver [third key insight or the "simple mental switch" / solution].
- This is the partial payoff for Tension Loop #1.

TENSION LOOP #2 (mid-body / ~15 words):
- Open a NEW question: "Now, this only works if you avoid one specific
  mistake that [X%] of people make..."
- Answer it in the next section.

PAYOFF / RESOLUTION:
- Close the original tension loop explicitly. Reference the hook detail.
- Give the viewer one actionable takeaway they can use TODAY.

CTA (final 15 seconds):
- Reference the pain point from the STAKES section.
- Frame the action as the viewer's next logical step in their own story.
- Example structure: "If you're tired of [pain from stakes], then
  [specific action] is the single smallest step. And if you want
  [deeper outcome], the video on your screen right now walks through
  [specific promise]."

--- VOICE CONSTRAINTS ---
- Sentence length: vary between 5 and 22 words. Never three long
  sentences in a row.
- Include exactly [1–2] sentence fragments for emphasis.
- Use contractions throughout. Write like you talk.
- One dry, observational joke (not a pun) placed after the stakes beat.
- Address the viewer as "you" at least [5] times in the first 90 seconds.
- NO phrases like: "In today's video," "Let's dive in," "Without further
  ado," "In this comprehensive guide."

--- OUTPUT FORMAT ---
- Write the full script with [SECTION HEADERS] in brackets.
- Include approximate timestamps in parentheses at the start of each section.
- After the script, add a 3-bullet "Structural Integrity Check" confirming
  that the Hook contains a specific detail, the Stakes reference a personal
  pain, and at least two Tension Loops are present and resolved.
```
