## TOOL 10: The 30-Day Structural Prompting Integration Plan

### *Because reading a chapter changes nothing. Doing the thing for 30 days changes everything.*

---

### Week 1: Demolition (Days 1–7)

**Goal:** Break the adjective habit. Make the Detail Fallacy visible in your own work.

| Day | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Complete Tool 1 (Autopsy Worksheet) on your most recent script. | 45–60 min |
| 2 | Complete Tool 2 (Rewrite Tax Calculator) on the same script. | 20 min |
| 3 | Complete Tool 3 (Detail Fallacy Spotter) on your last 3 prompts. | 30 min |
| 4 | Complete Tool 4 (Retention Gap Map) if you have analytics data. If not, skip. | 30 min |
| 5 | Write one new script using Prompt Script A, B, or C (Tool 9). Do NOT add any adjective-based instructions. | 60 min |
| 6 | Record and edit that script. Note how much structural repair you had to do vs. cosmetic. | Variable |
| 7 | **Reflection:** Write 3 sentences in your notes app. What surprised you? What felt uncomfortable? What's one thing you'll never do again? | 10 min |

---

### Week 2: Construction (Days 8–14)

**Goal:** Build the structural prompting reflex.

| Day | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 8 | Before writing ANY prompt, complete Tool 7 (Pre-Script Structural Checklist). All 10 boxes. | 5 min |
| 9 | Write a script using Tool 5 (Structural Prompt Builder). Fill every bracket. | 45 min |
| 10 | Run the output through Tool 6 (60-Second Hook Engineer). Score it. Fix if under 4/5. | 15 min |
| 11 | Edit the script. Use Tool 8 (Diagnostic Cards) for any section that feels off. Name the missing element before fixing it. | Variable |
| 12 | Write a second script on a different topic. Same process. | 45 min |
| 13 | Compare editing time on these two scripts vs. your Week 1 baseline (Tool 2). Calculate the % reduction. | 15 min |
| 14 | **Reflection:** What structural element do you still forget to prompt for? Write it on a sticky note. Put it on your monitor. | 5 min |

---

### Week 3: Refinement (Days 15–21)

**Goal:** Develop speed and instinct.

| Day | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 15–17 | Write 3 scripts using only structural prompts. No adjective instructions allowed. If you catch yourself writing "make it engaging," stop. Delete it. Replace with a structural beat. | 45 min each |
| 18 | Do a full Autopsy (Tool 1) on your best-performing video ever (or a creator you admire). Reverse-engineer their structure. What beats are they hitting? | 45 min |
| 19 | Adapt one structural pattern you found in Day 18 into your Prompt Script template. | 20 min |
| 20 | Write a script using your modified template. | 45 min |
| 21 | **Reflection:** Can you now look at a flat script and immediately name the missing structural element without referring to the cards? Test yourself: pull up an old script, read one section, diagnose it in 10 seconds. | 15 min |

---

### Week 4: Integration (Days 22–30)

**Goal:** Make this your default. No more "detail first" prompting. Ever.

| Day | Action | Time |
|---|---|---|
| 22–28 | Normal production schedule. But every prompt starts with Tool 7 (checklist) and uses Tool 5 or Tool 9 as the base. No exceptions. | 5 min pre-prompt |
| 29 | Pull retention data on the videos you made in Weeks 2–3. Compare to your pre-book baseline. Note the difference. Even a 5% improvement in 30-second retention is significant. | 20 min |
| 30 | **Final reflection:** Write a short "letter to your past self" — the one who spent four hours on a script at 2:47 p.m. on a Thursday, adding "make it more conversational" for the eleventh time. What do you know now that they didn't? | 15 min |

---

### The Non-Negotiable Rule (tape this somewhere visible)

> **I do not describe the feeling I want. I engineer the mechanism that produces it.**
>
> I don't say "make it engaging." I say "open with a specific number that contradicts an assumption."
>
> I don't say "add personality." I say "include one self-deprecating admission in the first 30 seconds."
>
> I don't say "build tension." I say "end this section with an unanswered question that won't be resolved until Section 3."
>
> Structure first. Voice second. Adjectives never.
