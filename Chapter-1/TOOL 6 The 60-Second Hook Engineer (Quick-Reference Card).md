## TOOL 6: The 60-Second Hook Engineer (Quick-Reference Card)

### *Before you write any script intro, run it through this 5-point check. If any box is unchecked, the hook isn't ready.*

**Working hook (paste your current draft):**

> _______________________________________________
> _______________________________________________

| Check | Question | Pass? |
|---|---|---|
| ☐ Specificity | Does the first sentence contain a concrete number, name, date, or sensory detail? (Not "many people" but "43 apps.") | ☐ Y ☐ N |
| ☐ Disruption | Does it contradict an assumption, break a pattern, or create a micro-mystery? (The viewer's scroll-thumb should hesitate.) | ☐ Y ☐ N |
| ☐ Vulnerability / Stakes | Within the first two sentences, does the viewer feel "this person is admitting something" or "this is about MY problem"? | ☐ Y ☐ N |
| ☐ Forward Pull | Is there a reason to keep watching embedded in the hook itself? (A promised revelation, an unfinished story, a "but here's the twist" energy.) | ☐ Y ☐ N |
| ☐ No Topic-Sentence Trap | Does the hook AVOID simply stating what the video is about? (If you can replace it with "In this video I'll talk about X" and lose nothing, it's a topic sentence, not a hook.) | ☐ Y ☐ N |

**Score: ___/5**

- 5/5 → Record it.
- 3–4/5 → Fix the missing element. Use the Structural Prompt Builder (Tool 5) to regenerate just the hook.
- 0–2/5 → Scrap it. You're looking at a topic sentence in a hook's clothing. Start over with a specific detail from your own experience.

### Hook Formula Menu (pick one per script)

| Formula | Template | Example |
|---|---|---|
| The Confession | "I [did embarrassing thing] and [specific number/result]. Here's what that taught me about [topic]." | "I spent $4,200 on courses I never finished. The pattern wasn't laziness—it was something the course creators were doing on purpose." |
| The Contradiction | "[Common belief]. I believed it for [time period]. Then [specific event] proved it completely wrong." | "Multitasking makes you more productive. I ran my business that way for three years. Then a single Tuesday afternoon cost me a $12K client." |
| The Number Drop | "Out of [specific total], only [shocking subset] [unexpected outcome]. And the reason isn't what you think." | "Out of 217 emails I sent last month, exactly 4 got replies. And the 4 that worked broke every 'professional email' rule I'd been taught." |
| The Pattern Interrupt | Start mid-action, mid-sentence, or mid-thought. Drop the viewer into a scene. | "I was 40 minutes into a recording, talking to nobody, when I realized I'd been scripting my videos like a college essay for two years." |
| The Direct Challenge | "You're [doing common thing]. And it's [specific negative consequence]. Not because you're [expected blame], but because [real cause]." | "You're rewriting your AI scripts five times before they sound right. And it's not because the AI is bad. It's because you're asking it to decorate a house that has no walls." |
