## 1. Structure-First Prompting Cheat Sheet

**Keep this one-pager next to your keyboard until the structural lens becomes automatic.**

| **Load-Bearing Element** | **What It Does** | **Prompt Command Examples** |
|--------------------------|------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Hook** | Interrupts the scroll within 3 seconds using curiosity, surprise, or recognition. | *“Open with a specific, counter-intuitive statistic that contradicts what the viewer assumes.”* <br> *“Start with a vulnerable personal confession that makes the viewer think, ‘I’ve felt that too.’”* |
| **Stakes** | Makes the topic feel personally urgent; answers “Why should I care *right now*?” within the first 10–15 seconds. | *“Immediately explain why this matters to anyone who edits video for a living.”* <br> *“Name the exact consequence of ignoring this problem—lost income, wasted time, damaged reputation.”* |
| **Tension Loop** | A promised answer, a pattern break, or an unanswered question that forces the viewer to stay until the end. | *“Pose a question that won’t be answered until the final section of the video.”* <br> *“Tease a ‘simple mental switch’ the viewer will learn, but don’t reveal it yet.”* |
| **Payoff** | Satisfies the tension loop in a way that feels earned, often with an actionable takeaway or emotional release. | *“Reveal the simple mental switch in a single sentence, then show exactly how to apply it today.”* <br> *“Close the loop by tying the ending back to the opening hook.”* |
| **Transitions** | Smooth, invisible bridges between beats that maintain momentum. | *“Use a single provocative sentence to pivot from the problem into the solution.”* <br> *“Carry the viewer from the personal anecdote into the broader research without losing energy.”* |

**Remember:** None of these are adjectives. They’re architectural instructions. If you find yourself writing “be more engaging,” stop and ask, “Which structural beam is missing?”
