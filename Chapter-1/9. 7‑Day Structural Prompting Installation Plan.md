## 9. 7‑Day Structural Prompting Installation Plan

A day‑by‑day guide to make the shift permanent. Tick off each day as you complete it.

**Day 1 – Autopsy**  
- [ ] Complete the AI Script Autopsy Worksheet on your most recent AI script.  
- [ ] Identify the single most repeated missing function (likely Stakes or Tension Loop).  
- [ ] Write one structural sentence that would have fixed it, and tape it to your monitor.

**Day 2 – Calculate the Tax**  
- [ ] Fill out the Rewrite Tax Calculator for the same script.  
- [ ] Note your structural repair percentage. If it’s above 60%, set a goal to halve it within a month.  
- [ ] Write down what you’ll do with the reclaimed hours.

**Day 3 – Spot the Fallacy**  
- [ ] Do the Detail Fallacy Spotter for your last three prompts.  
- [ ] Replace every vague instruction with a structural command.  
- [ ] Open a new chat and test the rewritten prompt on a fresh script intro. Compare with the old output.

**Day 4 – Map the Gap**  
- [ ] Complete the Retention Gap Map for the same video, if data is available.  
- [ ] Draw a direct line from each retention drop to a missing structural beat.  
- [ ] Write a one‑sentence “if I could go back” fix and save it.

**Day 5 – Practice the Blueprint**  
- [ ] Use the Structural Prompt Template (Section 5) to generate an intro for a completely new topic idea.  
- [ ] Do not edit the output—just score it: Hook present? Stakes? Tension Loop?  
- [ ] If any piece is missing, adjust the prompt and regenerate until all three are solid.

**Day 6 – Audit Your Old Intros**  
- [ ] Pull the intros from your last five videos (AI or not).  
- [ ] For each, label the Hook, Stakes, and Tension Loop—or note their absence.  
- [ ] Notice patterns. Do your older videos lack stakes entirely? Are your hooks actually just context?

**Day 7 – Identity Anchor**  
- [ ] Read the Daily Structural Lens Practice Script aloud, then rewrite it in your own words.  
- [ ] Record a voice memo explaining the Detail Fallacy as if to a creator friend. Teaching solidifies the shift.  
- [ ] Commit: “From now on, my prompts begin with architecture, not adjectives.”
