## Quick-Reference: The Chapter 1 Mental Model (One-Page Summary)

**The Lie:** Better scripts come from more detailed tone instructions.

**The Truth:** Better scripts come from explicit narrative architecture.

**The Detail Fallacy:** Vague stylistic prompts ("engaging," "conversational," "punchy") scatter the AI's attention across conflicting statistical patterns. The output is a compromise. Compromises don't retain viewers.

**The Structural Lens:** Every script is a sequence of psychological states. Your job is to engineer the transitions between those states:

> Curiosity spike → Personal identification → Stakes escalation → Partial resolution → New mystery → Deeper payoff → Action trigger

**The Three Load-Bearing Walls of Any Opening:**
1. **Hook** — interrupts the scroll (specific, surprising, concrete)
2. **Stakes** — makes it personal ("this is about YOU and YOUR pain")
3. **Tension Loop** — creates a forward pull (an unanswered question)

**The Script Engineer's Diagnostic Question:**
Not "Does this sound good?" but "What narrative function is this line serving, and is it serving it?"

**The Rewrite Tax:** The hours you spend manually fixing structural gaps that a structurally explicit prompt would have built correctly the first time.

**The Shift:** From decorating with adjectives → to engineering with architecture.
