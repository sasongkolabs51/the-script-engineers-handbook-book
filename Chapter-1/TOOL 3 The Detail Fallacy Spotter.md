### The Translation Cheat Sheet

Keep this handy. Left column = what you used to write. Right column = what you write now.

| Old Habit (Adjective Prompting) | New Habit (Structural Prompting) |
|---|---|
| "Make it engaging" | "Open with [specific surprising fact / personal confession / pattern interrupt] that creates a curiosity gap the viewer needs closed." |
| "Be more conversational" | "Write as if explaining this to a friend at a kitchen table. Use one incomplete sentence. Include one 'here's the thing' pivot. Address the viewer as 'you' at least three times in the first 60 seconds." |
| "Add personality" | "Include one self-deprecating anecdote (max 2 sentences) within the first 30 seconds. Use one unexpected metaphor that connects [topic] to [unrelated domain]." |
| "Sound natural" | "Vary sentence length: follow any sentence over 20 words with one under 8. Include one sentence fragment for emphasis. Avoid starting three consecutive sentences with the same word." |
| "Make it punchy" | "Cut every sentence to its core claim. No sentence should exceed 15 words in the hook section. Use active voice. Start the second sentence with a conjunction (But / And / So)." |
| "Add humor but keep it smart" | "Insert one dry, observational joke that relies on an unexpected comparison (not a pun). Place it immediately after the stakes statement to release tension before escalating again." |
| "Create a strong hook" | "The first sentence must contain a specific number, a personal admission, or a direct contradiction of a common belief. The viewer should feel slightly uncomfortable or deeply curious within 5 seconds." |
| "Build tension throughout" | "End every section with an unanswered question or a 'but here's what nobody tells you' pivot. Never resolve a tension loop without immediately opening a new one." |
| "Make the CTA compelling" | "The CTA must reference the specific pain point from the hook. Frame the action (subscribe / comment / watch next) as the logical next step in the viewer's own story, not a favor to you." |
