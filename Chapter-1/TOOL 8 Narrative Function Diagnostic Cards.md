## TOOL 8: Narrative Function Diagnostic Cards

### *When a script section feels "off" but you can't name why, use these cards to identify the missing structural element.*

**Print these. Tape them to your monitor. Use them during every editing session.**

---

**CARD 1: "This section feels flat / boring."**

Diagnosis: You're likely missing **Stakes** or a **Tension Loop**.

Ask yourself:
- Does the viewer know *why this matters to them personally* right now?
- Is there an unanswered question pulling them forward?
- Am I just *informing* without creating *need*?

Fix: Insert a "here's why this should bother you" beat. Or end the section with "But here's the part nobody warns you about..."

---

**CARD 2: "The hook doesn't grab."**

Diagnosis: You wrote a **Topic Sentence**, not a Hook.

Ask yourself:
- Can I replace my first line with "In this video, I'll discuss X" and lose nothing?
- Is there a specific, concrete, surprising detail in sentence one?
- Does the viewer feel anything in the first 5 seconds, or just register information?

Fix: Replace the topic sentence with a Confession, Contradiction, or Number Drop (see Tool 6).

---

**CARD 3: "The middle sags / viewers drop off."**

Diagnosis: You resolved your only Tension Loop too early and didn't open a new one.

Ask yourself:
- Where did I answer the question I posed in the intro?
- Did I immediately open a NEW question after answering it?
- Is there an escalation in stakes between section 2 and section 3?

Fix: Split your payoff. Give 70% of the answer, then pivot: "But this only works if you avoid [new mystery]." Answer the new mystery in the next section.

---

**CARD 4: "The CTA feels tacked-on / robotic."**

Diagnosis: The CTA isn't connected to the **emotional journey** of the script.

Ask yourself:
- Does my CTA reference the specific pain I named in the Stakes beat?
- Am I asking the viewer to do something, or am I showing them the *next step in their own story*?
- Would this CTA make sense if the viewer skipped to the end without watching?

Fix: Rewrite the CTA to start with "If you're still [pain from intro]..." and frame the action as the smallest possible next step toward relief.

---

**CARD 5: "The whole script sounds like a Wikipedia article with a smile."**

Diagnosis: You prompted for **tone** instead of **structure**. The Detail Fallacy in action.

Ask yourself:
- Did my prompt contain more adjectives than structural commands?
- Did I tell the AI *how to feel* instead of *what to build*?
- Is there a single specific, personal, concrete detail in the entire script?

Fix: Scrap the prompt. Rebuild using Tool 5. Replace every "make it X" instruction with a structural beat.
