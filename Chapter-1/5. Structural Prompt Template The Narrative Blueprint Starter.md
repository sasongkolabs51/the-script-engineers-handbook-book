## 5. Structural Prompt Template: The Narrative Blueprint Starter

**Purpose:** A fill‑in template that forces you to engineer Hook, Stakes, and Tension Loop before you ask for anything else. Use this as the base for every script intro you generate from now on.

```
Write a YouTube script intro for a video about [TOPIC].

STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS (follow these exactly; do not deviate):

1. HOOK (first 1–2 sentences): Start with a specific, unexpected fact, number, or personal admission that contradicts a common belief about [TOPIC]. This must create immediate curiosity or surprise. Do NOT ask a rhetorical question. Do NOT say "Have you ever…" or "In today's video…"

2. STAKES (within the first 10 seconds): Make it clear why this matters urgently to the viewer. Connect the hook to a real consequence the viewer might face—wasted time, lost money, a mistake they don't know they're making. Use "you" or "your" to make it personal.

3. TENSION LOOP (end of intro, before the first section): Pose a specific, unanswered question or promise a revelation that will only be resolved later in the video. This must be concrete enough that the viewer feels they cannot get the answer without watching the rest. Do NOT reveal the answer yet.

TONE: Write as if you're talking to one person, not a crowd. Keep sentences short and punchy. Use contractions. Avoid any sentence that sounds like an article headline.

OUTPUT ONLY THE INTRO (approximately 30–60 seconds when spoken).
```

*After generating, check the output against the three structural requirements before accepting it. If any requirement is missing, do not edit the script—fix the prompt and regenerate.*
