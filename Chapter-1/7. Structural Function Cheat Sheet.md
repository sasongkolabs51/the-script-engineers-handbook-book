## 7. Structural Function Cheat Sheet

Keep this nearby when you write prompts. It’s the minimal set of narrative functions every retention‑optimized script needs.

| Function | What It Does | Prompt Instruction Snippet |
|----------|--------------|----------------------------|
| **Hook** | Interrupts the scroll; makes the viewer lean in. | “Start with a specific, counter‑intuitive fact or personal admission. No rhetorical questions.” |
| **Stakes** | Makes the topic feel urgent and personally relevant. | “Within 10 seconds, explain what the viewer stands to lose or gain. Use ‘you’ and a concrete consequence.” |
| **Tension Loop** | Creates a knowledge gap that can only be closed by watching further. | “Pose a specific question or promise a revelation that won’t be resolved until later in the video. Do not answer it yet.” |
| **Payoff** | Delivers the promised insight or resolution. | “Explicitly tie the answer back to the tension loop. Show how the viewer’s problem is now solved or understood.” |
| **Transition** | Moves the viewer smoothly to the next section without losing momentum. | “Bridge by referencing the previous point and hinting at what’s next. Avoid ‘Now let’s talk about…’—make it feel like a natural progression.” |
| **CTA** | Converts retained attention into action (subscribe, comment, next video). | “Link the CTA to the emotional journey the viewer just experienced. For example, ‘If you’ve ever lost hours to… then the next video is for you.’” |
