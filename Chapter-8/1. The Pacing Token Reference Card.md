## 1. The Pacing Token Reference Card

Print this and keep it next to your monitor. Every token in this list is a command you can embed directly into your script prompt. The AI will reproduce it faithfully, turning your text into a scored performance.

### Pause Tokens

| Token | Function | Typical Placement | Example |
|-------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| `[Pause 0.5s]` | Micro‑pause; separates setup from punchline or quick thought shift. | Between two short sentences where a tiny breath adds impact. | “I checked the stats again. [Pause 0.5s] They were worse than I feared.” |
| `[Pause 1.5s]` | Standard beat; lets a significant statement land. | After a key revelation, a rhetorical question, or a major transition. | “That’s when I realized what was actually happening. [Pause 1.5s] I wasn’t bad at shipping projects.” |
| `[Pause 3s]` | Dramatic silence; used after a profound revelation or before a tonal shift. | After a rock‑bottom moment in the narrative, or right before the solution pivot. | “I had lost the client. [Pause 3s] But that failure taught me something nothing else could.” |

### Speed Tokens

| Token | Function | Typical Placement | Example |
|-------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| `[Fast energy]` | Rapid, excited delivery. | At the hook, during agitation escalations, or when building momentum toward a discovery. | `[Fast energy]` “I opened the email, and my heart rate doubled. The subject line read…” |
| `[Slow down]` | Decelerate for emphasis or emotional weight. | During a personal confession, the introduction of the core insight, or a reflective closing. | `[Slow down]` “What I’m about to say took me five years to understand.” |
| `[Build speed]` | Gradual acceleration over a sentence or two. | Leading into a climax or before a big reveal. | `[Build speed]` “First the downloads dropped, then the comments dried up, then the unsubscribes started rolling in.” |

### Emphasis Tokens

| Token | Function | Typical Placement | Example |
|-------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| `[Whisper]` | For a secret, vulnerable admission, or conspiratorial aside. | Precedes a short phrase you want delivered in a hushed, intimate tone. | “And then I heard something I’ll never forget. [Whisper] ‘You’re not good enough.’” |
| `[Punch]` | Sharp emphasis on a key word or punchline. | Right before the critical word in a joke or the core number in a statistic. | “I spent [Punch] twelve thousand dollars testing every project management tool on the market.” |
| `[Slow emphasis]` | Slow, deliberate enunciation of a word or phrase for weight. | Around a product name, a central concept, or a life‑changing word. | “That word is [Slow emphasis] resonance. And it changed everything.” |

### Visual Tokens

| Token | Function | Typical Placement | Example |
|-------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| `[B‑roll moment: describe X]` | Indicates where a visual cutaway should happen, and what it should show. | Every 90–120 seconds of spoken content; when you reference a statistic, a tool, or a scene. | “The dashboard hadn’t changed in six years. [B‑roll moment: split‑screen of the 2019 and 2025 interfaces, highlighting identical elements.]” |
| `[Reaction beat]` | A moment where the presenter looks at the camera silently, reacting. | After a vulnerable confession, a shocking revelation, or a joke that needs a beat to land. | “I realized I’d been the problem all along. [Reaction beat]” |
| `[Text on screen: “quote”]` | Specifies on‑screen text that reinforces a key line. | For statistics, memorable quotes, or the core takeaway. | “The average user quits after day three. [Text on screen: ‘Day 3 — The Cliff’]” |

### Transition Tokens

| Token | Function | Typical Placement | Example |
|-------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| `[Hard cut]` | Abrupt shift in topic or tone, often for comedic effect. | Between two contrasting ideas or to break tension suddenly. | “So that’s the official story. [Hard cut] Here’s what actually happened.” |
| `[Fade out energy]` | Gradual deceleration toward a calm, reflective close. | In the final 30–60 seconds of the video, leading into the CTA. | `[Fade out energy]` “And that’s the quiet truth I wish I’d known a decade ago.” |
| `[Beat drop]` | The moment after a long pause when energy or music returns, signaling a new section. | After a dramatic silence, when you re‑engage with a fresh energy level. | `[Beat drop]` “So what do you actually do about it? Let’s break it down.” |
