## TOOL 1: The Pacing Token Reference Card

### *Your complete rhythmic vocabulary. Fifteen tokens, five categories. Keep this open during every Layer 3 session.*

---

### Category 1: Pause Tokens (Silence)

| Token | Duration | When to Use | Placement Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `[Pause 0.5s]` | Micro-beat | Before a punchline word; after a setup; separating two contrasting ideas | Immediately before the word/phrase it sets up | "The best productivity system is [Pause 0.5s] the one you'll actually use." |
| `[Pause 1.5s]` | Standard beat | After a significant statement; before a pivot; letting a key insight land | After the statement, before the next thought | "I wasn't bad at shipping. [Pause 1.5s] I was addicted to almost finishing." |
| `[Pause 3s]` | Dramatic silence | After a revelation; before a major tonal shift; at the emotional peak | After the biggest moment. Let it breathe. Don't rush the next line. | "And that's when I understood what I'd actually lost. [Pause 3s] It wasn't the client." |

**Pause Rules:**
- Pauses go BEFORE revelations, not after. The silence creates anticipation.
- Maximum 2 pauses per 100 words. More = halting, not dramatic.
- Never place a pause between a subject and its verb. ("I [Pause] realized" = wrong.)
- A [Pause 3s] should appear at most 1–2 times per entire script. It's the nuclear option.

---

### Category 2: Speed Tokens (Tempo)

| Token | Effect | When to Use | Placement Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `[Fast energy]` | Accelerate delivery; shorter sentences; urgency | Hooks; lists; building momentum; "here's what happened next" moments | Prefixes a section of 2–4 sentences. The AI compresses sentence length. | "[Fast energy] Three apps. Three weeks. Three crashes. Same pattern. Every. Single. Time." |
| `[Slow down]` | Decelerate; longer sentences; reflective weight | Vulnerable admissions; reframes; "here's what I learned" moments; solutions | Prefixes a section of 2–3 sentences. The AI expands sentence length. | "[Slow down] And I think the reason we don't talk about this is because admitting it means admitting we built our entire identity on a system that was never designed to love us back." |
| `[Build speed]` | Crescendo; gradually accelerating over 2–3 sentences | Leading into a climax; building toward a reveal; escalation sequences | Prefixes the first sentence of a 2–3 sentence acceleration sequence. | "[Build speed] At first it was one notification. Then three. Then the phone was buzzing every ninety seconds and I couldn't— [Pause 0.5s] —think." |

**Speed Rules:**
- Every [Fast energy] section needs a [Slow down] or [Pause] within 3–4 sentences. You can't sprint forever.
- [Build speed] must resolve into either a [Pause] (for impact) or a [Hard cut] (for surprise).
- Never use [Fast energy] in reflective/vulnerable sections. Never use [Slow down] in hooks.

---

### Category 3: Emphasis Tokens (Delivery)

| Token | Effect | When to Use | Placement Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `[Whisper]` | Drop volume; intimacy; secrecy | Sharing insider knowledge; a confession; a "just between us" moment | Prefixes the specific sentence or phrase to be whispered. Max 1–2 per script section. | "[Whisper] And here's the part they don't put in the documentation..." |
| `[Punch]` | Hit one word/phrase hard; stress it | The key word in a punchline; the core number in a stat; the turning-point word | Wraps the specific word or short phrase. Never more than 3 words. | "The error wasn't in the code. It was in the [Punch] assumption." |
| `[Slow emphasis]` | Savor a term; stretch it slightly | A product name; a central concept; a term the viewer needs to remember | Wraps the specific term. Max 1 per section. | "What you're actually building isn't a routine. It's a [Slow emphasis] identity." |

**Emphasis Rules:**
- [Punch] wraps max 3 words. Never a full sentence.
- [Whisper] max 1–2 times per script section. Overuse kills intimacy.
- [Slow emphasis] max 1 per section. If everything is emphasized, nothing is.

---

### Category 4: Visual Tokens (Production Cues)

| Token | Effect | When to Use | Placement Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `[B-roll: describe specific visual]` | Visual cutaway; illustration moment | After a claim needing evidence; during a list; at a transition; when the viewer needs to SEE something | At natural breaks between complete thoughts. Never mid-sentence. Every 90–120 seconds of spoken content. | "[B-roll: Split-screen of my 2019 vs. 2025 dashboard — identical layout, same color scheme, same three widgets]" |
| `[Reaction beat]` | Presenter reacts physically (face, gesture, silence) | After something absurd; during a "can you believe this?" moment; after a personal confession | After the statement that triggers the reaction. 1–2 seconds of non-verbal response. | "They charged me $200 for the 'premium' tier. [Reaction beat] *stares at camera, slight head shake*" |
| `[Text on screen: "quote"]` | Key line reinforced visually | A crucial statistic; a memorable one-liner; a framework name; a step in a process | Immediately after the spoken line it reinforces. | "The rule is simple: [Text on screen: 'If it takes 2 minutes, do it now.'] two minutes or less, do it immediately." |

**Visual Rules:**
- [B-roll] descriptions must be SPECIFIC: brand names, interface elements, specific objects, colors. Not "show a laptop." Instead: "close-up of a MacBook Pro M3 with 47 Chrome tabs open, fan visibly spinning."
- [B-roll] every 90–120 seconds of spoken content. More = choppy. Less = visually stagnant.
- [Reaction beat] pairs naturally with [Pause 1.5s] or [Pause 2s]. The silence IS the reaction.
- [Text on screen] max 3–4 per script. If everything is on screen, nothing is memorable.

---

### Category 5: Transition Tokens (Structural Shifts)

| Token | Effect | When to Use | Placement Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `[Hard cut]` | Abrupt tonal/topical shift; often comedic | After a long serious section, pivoting to something unexpected; a joke after heavy content; a sudden change of direction | Between the last sentence of one section and the first of the next. No bridge. Just... cut. | "...and that's how I lost $12,000 in a single afternoon. [Hard cut] Anyway. Let's talk about email subject lines." |
| `[Fade out energy]` | Decrescendo; winding down; leading to close | The final 2–3 sentences before the CTA; the emotional resolution; the "landing" | Prefixes the final reflective section. Sentences get slightly longer, softer. | "[Fade out energy] And honestly? That's enough. You don't need the perfect system. You just need the one you'll open on a Tuesday morning when you're tired and it still works." |
| `[Beat drop]` | Energy returns after a pause/silence; new section begins | After a [Pause 3s]; after a long reflective section; signaling "okay, new energy now" | After the pause/silence. The first sentence after a [Beat drop] should be short and punchy. | "[Pause 3s] [Beat drop] So. What do you actually DO with this?" |

**Transition Rules:**
- [Hard cut] max 1–2 per script. It's a spice, not a staple.
- [Fade out energy] appears once, in the final section. Never mid-script.
- [Beat drop] always follows a [Pause 2s] or [Pause 3s]. It's the "inhale" after the silence.

---

### Token Density Guidelines

| Script Length | Total Tokens (approx.) | Pauses | Speed | Emphasis | Visual | Transition |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3 min (~450 words) | 8–12 | 3–4 | 2–3 | 1–2 | 2–3 | 0–1 |
| 8 min (~1,200 words) | 18–25 | 6–8 | 4–6 | 3–4 | 5–7 | 1–2 |
| 12 min (~1,800 words) | 25–35 | 8–12 | 6–8 | 4–6 | 7–10 | 2–3 |
| 20 min (~3,000 words) | 40–55 | 12–18 | 8–12 | 6–8 | 12–15 | 2–3 |

**The Golden Rule:** If you're placing a token on more than 30% of sentences, you're over-tokenizing. Tokens mark *inflection points*, not every beat. The unmarked sentences are the "normal" rhythm that makes the marked ones pop.
