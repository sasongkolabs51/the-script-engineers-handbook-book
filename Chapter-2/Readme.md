## How to Use This Toolkit

This chapter guides you through the process of unlearning. Unlearning is more challenging than learning a new skill because you must dismantle a behavior that *feels* productive even when it isn’t. The exercises ahead are designed to make that unlearning tangible—you will feel the shift, not just think about it.

Think of unlearning as retraining a muscle. Your old prompting habit developed for a reason, and now you need to guide it toward a new pattern. Each tool in this section pushes you out of familiar territory so the new instinct can take root.

**Suggested order:**

- **Start with Tool 1** (Vault Inventory Audit). Consider this your cold splash of reality. Complete it before anything else. Let the raw numbers make the problem impossible to ignore.
- **Move to Tool 3** (Output Gap Calculator) while the sting of the audit is still sharp. Attach a dollar figure to that feeling of being stuck on a plateau.
- **Tool 2** (Principle Extraction Protocol) becomes your new permanent habit. Practice it on five templates pulled straight from your vault.
- **Tool 4** (Personal Prompt Lab) is where you will spend most of your time from now on. Build it today. Immediate action transforms intention into momentum.
- **Tool 5** (Experiment Templates) provides structured formats for your lab sessions.
- **Tool 6** (The Save Filter) is a ten‑second gate you run before saving any new material.
- **Tool 7** (Worked Examples) shows you what effective principle extraction looks like. Study these before attempting your own.
- **Tool 8** (The Pledge) acts as your circuit breaker. Write it. Post it. Mean it.
- **Tool 9** (30‑Day Workshop Plan) is your integration schedule. Follow it faithfully.
- **Tool 10** (Quick‑Reference Cards) belong on your wall or monitor. They serve as constant, gentle reminders.

One rule governs this entire toolkit: nothing gets saved without being tested. If you read a template here and think, “I’ll use this later,” you have already slipped back into Secret‑Seeker mode. Use it today. On a real topic. In your Prompt Lab. Within the hour.
