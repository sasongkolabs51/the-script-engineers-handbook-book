## TOOL 9: The 30-Day Workshop Practice Plan

### *Thirty days of deliberate practice to permanently replace the collection habit with the craftsmanship habit.*

---

### Week 1: The Purge (Days 1–7)

**Theme:** Confront the collection. Extract what's useful. Release the rest.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Complete Tool 1 (Vault Inventory Audit). Every item. No skipping. | 60–90 min | ☐ |
| 2 | Complete Tool 3 (Output Gap Calculator). Let the number land. Sit with it. | 30 min | ☐ |
| 3 | Pick your 5 best templates from the audit. Run each through Tool 2 (Principle Extraction Protocol). | 60 min | ☐ |
| 4 | Delete or archive everything you marked "Discard" in the audit. Close those browser tabs. Unsubscribe from the "prompt of the day" newsletters. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 5 | Set up your Personal Prompt Lab (Tool 4). Build the actual space. Three sections. Experiment log. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 6 | Run your first experiment in the lab. Use Experiment Format A (Structure Isolation). One topic, three hook structures. | 30 min | ☐ |
| 7 | **Weekly reflection:** Write 5 sentences. What did the audit reveal? What felt uncomfortable? What's one principle you extracted that you now own? | 15 min | ☐ |

---

### Week 2: The Build (Days 8–14)

**Theme:** Establish the daily lab habit. One experiment per day.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8 | Lab experiment: Structure Isolation Test on a real topic from your calendar. | 25 min | ☐ |
| 9 | Lab experiment: Voice Vector Test. Same topic, three voice profiles. | 25 min | ☐ |
| 10 | Lab experiment: Pacing Spike Test. Same section, three pacing approaches. Read all three aloud. | 25 min | ☐ |
| 11 | Write a full script using ONLY structural prompts (no adjective instructions). Use the principles you extracted this week. | 50 min | ☐ |
| 12 | Edit that script. Track: how much structural repair vs. cosmetic? Compare to your Tool 2 baseline. | Variable | ☐ |
| 13 | Encounter a new template/hack online (you will—algorithms will serve it to you). Run Tool 6 (Save Filter). Then Tool 2 if it passes. | 15 min | ☐ |
| 14 | **Weekly reflection:** Update your Experiment Log. What principles are solidifying? Where do you still feel the pull to "just find a template"? | 15 min | ☐ |

---

### Week 3: The Stress Test (Days 15–21)

**Theme:** Apply the workshop under real production pressure.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15–17 | Produce 2–3 scripts for your actual channel using only your Prompt Lab principles. No template browsing. No "quick search for a hook formula." Build from structure. | 45 min each | ☐ |
| 18 | Run a "blind test": write a hook using your principles, then write one using an old saved template. Read both aloud. Which has more structural integrity? Which would you actually use? | 20 min | ☐ |
| 19 | Teach the Principle Extraction Protocol to someone (a friend, a community post, a voice memo to yourself). Teaching forces clarity. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 20 | Deliberately encounter a "viral prompt" video or post. Notice the pull. Notice the dopamine anticipation. Then close it. Open your lab instead. Run one experiment. | 25 min | ☐ |
| 21 | **Weekly reflection:** Am I faster now? Is the quality more consistent? Do I feel less anxiety before starting a script because I trust my process? Write it down. | 15 min | ☐ |

---

### Week 4: The Integration (Days 22–30)

**Theme:** Make the workshop your default identity.

| Day | Action | Time | Done? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 22–27 | Normal production. But every session starts with: (1) Pre-Script Structural Checklist from Ch. 1, (2) one 10-min lab warm-up experiment, (3) structural prompt writing. No template browsing. | 10 min warm-up + production | ☐ |
| 28 | Re-run Tool 1 (Vault Audit) on whatever remains. Has the collection shrunk? Has usage gone up? | 20 min | ☐ |
| 29 | Write a "Principles I Own" list. Every principle you've extracted and tested in 30 days. This is your actual skill inventory. Not templates. Principles. | 20 min | ☐ |
| 30 | Revisit your Pledge (Tool 8). Read it aloud. Renew it or revise it. Write a short note to yourself: what would you tell the person who opened their Notion vault that morning in the chapter's opening scene? | 15 min | ☐ |

---

### The Daily Minimum (after Day 30, forever)

You don't need a 30-day plan forever. You need a daily minimum that keeps the workshop alive:

> **Every day I write a script:**
> - 5 minutes: one micro-experiment in the lab (test one variable on one section)
> - 2 minutes: write the principle I confirmed or revised
> - 0 minutes: browsing for templates
>
> **Every time I encounter a new technique:**
> - Tool 6 (Save Filter): 10 seconds
> - Tool 2 (Principle Extraction): 10 minutes if it passes
> - Immediate test: 5 minutes
>
> **Weekly:**
> - One full Structure Isolation or Voice Vector experiment (25 min)
> - Update Experiment Log
> - Check: did I save anything this week without extracting? If yes, do the extraction now.
