**My Secret-Seeker Pledge**

I, _________________________________, acknowledge that I have spent _________ (months/years) collecting templates, prompts, and "secrets" in the belief that the next one would unlock my growth. I acknowledge that this collection has produced _________% actual usage and _________ measurable improvement in my work.

I acknowledge that the act of saving felt like progress but was not progress. I acknowledge that my vault grew while my craft stayed still.

**Starting today, I commit to the following:**

1. I will not save any template, prompt, or technique without first extracting its underlying principle using the three-question protocol.

2. I will not save anything I cannot test within 48 hours. If I can't test it, I don't need it.

3. When I feel the urge to search for a "better prompt" or a "secret formula," I will instead open my Prompt Lab and run one deliberate experiment on the problem in front of me.

4. I will measure my growth by the principles I can articulate from memory, not by the number of files in my collection.

5. I accept that mastery is slow, iterative, and sometimes boring. I accept that the discomfort of productive struggle is the feeling of actual growth, and that the dopamine of a new download is not.

6. I will revisit this pledge on the 1st of every month. If I've slipped back into collection mode, I will not beat myself up. I will simply open my Prompt Lab and run one experiment. Then another. Then another.
