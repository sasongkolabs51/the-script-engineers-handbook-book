### Step 4: The Lab Rules (tape these to your monitor)

> 1. **One variable at a time.** Don't change the hook AND the voice AND the pacing in the same test. You'll learn nothing.
>
> 2. **Same topic, different lever.** Run the same content through different structural choices. The topic is the control; the structure is the variable.
>
> 3. **Read it aloud.** Every output gets read out loud before you judge it. Your ear catches what your eyes miss.
>
> 4. **Write the principle, not the output.** After each experiment, write one sentence: "I learned that [principle]." The generated text is disposable. The principle is yours forever.
>
> 5. **No saving. No hoarding. No "I'll use this later."** If a test produces something great, extract the principle (Tool 2) and move on. The lab is a gym, not a warehouse.
>
> 6. **Twenty minutes minimum, sixty minutes maximum.** Shorter than twenty and you haven't gone deep enough. Longer than sixty and you're procrastinating on real production.
