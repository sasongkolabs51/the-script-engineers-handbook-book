## 4. Personal Prompt Lab Setup Template

**Purpose:** Build the dedicated space where deliberate practice replaces random template testing. Your lab becomes the default destination for curiosity and frustration alike.

**Step 1 – Create the Container**  
Open a new document (Notion, Google Doc, or a dedicated AI conversation thread). Name it: **My Prompt Lab**.

**Step 2 – Insert the Three Sections**  
Copy and paste these section headers into your lab:

- **Structural Experiments**  
  (Test hook architectures, tension loops, CTA mechanics, etc.)  
- **Voice Experiments**  
  (Test tone profiles, word‑choice vectors, emotional register shifts.)  
- **Pacing Experiments**  
  (Test line length, pause placements, energy‑burst markers.)

**Step 3 – Log Your First Experiment**  
Fill out this card for the first experiment you’ll run today.

| Experiment Name | Section (Structural/Voice/Pacing) | Variable I’m Testing | Topic / Sample Script | Prompt I’ll Use (structural only, no adjectives) | Expected Outcome | Actual Outcome (after generation) | Lesson Learned |
|-----------------|-----------------------------------|----------------------|------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------|----------------|
| e.g., “Curiosity vs. Contrarian Hook” | Structural | Hook architecture | Productivity tool video | “Generate two intros on the same topic: one with a specific surprising stat, one with a bold contrarian claim. Do not add any other differences.” | The stat-based hook will feel more authoritative. | | |
